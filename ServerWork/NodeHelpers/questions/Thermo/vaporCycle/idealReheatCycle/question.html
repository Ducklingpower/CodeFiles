Steam is the working fluid in an ideal Rankine cycle with superheat and reheat. Steam enters the first-stage turbine at $\mathbf{P_1} = {{params.p1}}~MPa$, $\mathbf{T_1} = {{params.t1}}~^\circ C$, and expands to $\mathbf{P_2} = {{params.p2}}~MPa$. It is then reheated to $\mathbf{T_r} = {{params.tr}}~^\circ C$ before entering the second-stage turbine, where it expands to the condenser pressure of $\mathbf{P_3} = {{params.p3}}~MPa$. The net power output is $\mathbf{\dot{W}_{net} } = {{params.w_net}}~MW$.

Determine:

  • (a) The thermal efficiency of the cycle
  • (b) The mass flow rate of steam, in $\mathbf{kg/h}$
  • (c) The rate of heat transfer $\mathbf{\dot{Q}_{out} }$ from the condensing steam as it passes through the condenser, in $\mathbf{MW}$
