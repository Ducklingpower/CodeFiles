Steam is the working fluid in an ideal Rankine cycle. Saturated vapor enters the turbine at a pressure of $\mathbf{P_{turbine\_in} } = {{params.P_turbine_in}}~MPa$ and saturated liquid exits the condenser at a pressure of $\mathbf{P_{condenser\_out} } = {{params.P_condenser_out}}~MPa$.

Consider the vapor power cycle with the turbine and pump having an isentropic efficiency of $\mathbf{n} = {{params.efficiency}}$%. A net power output of $\mathbf{W_{net} } = {{params.netpower}}~MW$ is given. The cooling water enters the condenser at $\mathbf{T_{in} } = {{params.cooling_water_in}}~^{\circ}C$ and exits at $\mathbf{T_{out} } = {{params.cooling_water_out}}~^{\circ}C$. Steam enters the boiler and undergoes the Rankine Cycle.

Determine the following:

  • (a) The thermal efficiency of the cycle, $\eta$
  • (b) The mass flow rate of steam, $\mathbf{\dot{m}_{steam} }~(kg/h)$
  • (c) The rate of heat transfer into the working fluid, $\mathbf{\dot{Q}_{in} }~(MW)$
  • (d) The rate of heat transfer out from the condenser, $\mathbf{\dot{Q}_{out} }~(MW)$
  • (e) The mass flow rate of the condenser cooling water, $\mathbf{\dot{m}_{cooling~water} }~(kg/h)$
