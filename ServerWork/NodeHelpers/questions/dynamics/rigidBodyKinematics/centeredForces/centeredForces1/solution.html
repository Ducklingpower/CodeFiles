First draw a free body diagram. There should be four forces acting on the ring.

Drawing the free body diagram you will have weight, normal, friction, and tension.

  • Weight pulling down from the center
  • Normal pushing from the ground up
  • Tension force pulling the ring on the rope
  • Friction force pulling left from the contact area of the ring and the ground

    • Now that you have the free body diagram, you can write equations of motion to solve for unknowns.

      Equations of Motion:

      $\Sigma{F_x} = ma ~\implies~ ma = Tcos(\theta) - \mu_kN$

      $\Sigma{F_y} = 0 ~\implies~ 0 = Tsin(\theta) -w+N ~\implies~ N = w - Tsin(\theta)$

      plugging in $N = w - Tsin(\theta)$ into our $F_x$ equation we get:

    • $ma = Tcos(\theta) - \mu_k(w - Tsin(\theta)) ~\implies ~ ma = Tcos(\theta) - \mu_kw + \mu_kTsin(\theta)$

      • Here we have 1 equation and 2 unknowns, (a and T). We need one more equation.

      Moment equation:

      $\Sigma {M_{cg} } = 0 ~\implies~ 0 = rTsin(\theta-\phi) - \mu_k N ~\implies~ 0 = rTsin(\theta-\phi) - \mu_kwr+ \mu_krTsin(\theta) $

      Solving for tension we get:

    • $T = \frac{\mu_k r w}{rsin(\theta-\phi)+\mu_k r sin(\theta)}$

      • Plugging in tension into $ma = Tcos(\theta) - \mu_kw + \mu_kTsin(\theta)$ and solving for a we get:

    • $a = \frac{\mu_kr w cos(\theta)}{m(rsin(\theta-\phi) + \mu r sin(\theta))} - \frac{\mu_k w }{m} + \frac{\mu_k r w sin(\theta) \mu_k}{m(r sin(\theta-\phi) + \mu_k r sin(\theta))}$ = {{correct_answers.a}} $m/s^2$
