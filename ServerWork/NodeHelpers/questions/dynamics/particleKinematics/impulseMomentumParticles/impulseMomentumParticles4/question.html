The bullet of mass $\mathbf{m_B} = {{params.mb}} ~kg $ is travelling with a velocity of $\mathbf{v_B} = {{params.vb}}~m/s$ collides with the stationary block of mass $\mathbf{m} = {{params.m}}~kg$.

If the bullet sticks to the block after the collision, determine the final speed of the block right after the collision.

Also compute the time it takes for the block to come to a complete rest again and the total distance traveled, if the coefficient of kinetic friction is $\mathbf{\mu_k} = {{params.mu}}$.

Use conservation of impulse moments to solve this problem.
