To calculate the monthly payment and total interest paid on a mortgage, we can use the formula for the monthly payment on an amortizing loan. The formula for the monthly payment (M) is given by: \[ M = P \frac{r(1 + r)^n}{(1 + r)^n - 1} \] where:
  • P = principal amount (initial loan amount)
  • r = monthly interest rate (annual interest rate divided by 12)
  • n = number of payments (loan term in months)

  • P = {{params.principal}}
  • r = $\frac{ {{params.interestRate}} }{100 \cdot 12}$
  • n = ${{params.years}} \cdot 12$
 Substituting these values into the formula gives: \[ M = {{params.principal}} \frac{\frac{{params.interestRate}}{100 \cdot 12} (1 + \frac{{params.interestRate}}{100 \cdot 12} )^{({{params.years}} \times 12)} }{(1 + \frac{{params.interestRate}}{100 \cdot 12} )^{({{params.years}} \times 12)} - 1} \] The total amount paid over the life of the mortgage is given by: \[ \text{Total Payments} = M \times n \] Therefore, the total interest paid can be calculated as: \[ \text{Total Interest} = \text{Total Payments} - P \] Using the values calculated, we can find the monthly payment and total interest paid over the life of the mortgage.
