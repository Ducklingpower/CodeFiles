Level 0

The first step is to write out the equation of relative velocity and identify your unknowns.

Level 1

Reltive velocity equation: \( v_b = v_c + \omega _{BC} \) x \( r_{CB} \)

There will not be any velocity in the y direction since rod AB is perfectly verticle.
  • pluggig in we get: \( -\omega_{AB}L_1 \hat{i} = v_c \hat{j} + \omega_{BC} \) x \( [L_2cos(\theta)\hat{i} - L_2sin(\theta)\hat{j}] \)
  • Simplifying we get \( : -\omega_{AB}L_1 \hat{i} = v_c \hat{j} + \omega_{BC}L_2cos(\theta)\hat{j} +\omega_{BC}L_2 L_2sin(\theta)\hat{i} \)

    • Breaking up vector equation into components:

  • \( -\omega_{AB}L_1 \hat{i} = \omega_{BC} L_2 L_2sin(\theta)\hat{i} \)
  • \( 0 = v_c \hat{j} + \omega_{BC}L_2cos(\theta)\hat{j} \)

    • Now we have 2 equations and 2 unknowns.

    Level 2

      Solving the 2 equations we can obtain the angular velocity of both rods.

    • \( \omega_{BC} = -\frac{v}{L_2 cos(\theta)} = 68.3923473950016~rad/s \)
    • \( \omega_{AB} = tan(\theta)\frac{v}{L_1} = 108.72726723222299~rad/s \)

      • For this question the direction does not matter, only the magnitude is needed
