Level 0

For this problem it is best to write out the equation for solving \( \omega_{CD} \). Then we can identify what we have and what we need to solve.

Level 1

The equation for solving the velocity of point D:

  • \( \vec{v_D} = \vec{v_c} + \omega_{CD} \) x \( \vec{L_3} \)

    • Here we know the velocity at point D is zero since it is locked in place. We want \( \omega_{CD} \) and we don't know what \( \vec{v_c} \) is. So before we can solve for \( \omega_{CD} \), we have to first find what \( \vec{v_c} \) is.

    Level 2

      Solving for \( \vec{v_c} \): We can write out an equation that solves for \( \vec{v_c} \) similar to how we wrote out the equation for \( \vec{v_D} \).
    • \( \vec{v_c} = \vec{v_B} + \omega_{BC} \) x \( \vec{L_2} \)

      • Where \( \vec{v_B} \) = \( \omega_A \) x \( L_1 \) = \( -\omega_A L_1~\hat{j} \)

      Taking the cross product and plugging in \( \vec{v_B} \)

    • \( \vec{v_c} = L_2sin(\theta)\omega_{BC}~\hat{i}-\omega_AL_1~\hat{j}-L_2cos(\theta)\omega_{BC}~\hat{j} \)
      • Now we can plug in \( \vec{v_c} \) into \( \vec{v_D} = \vec{v_c} + \omega_{CD} \) x \( \vec{L_3} \). So that we can solve for \( \omega_{CD} \)

    Level 3

      Plugging in \( \vec{v_c} \) into \( \vec{v_D} = \vec{v_c} + \omega_{CD} \) x \( \vec{L_3} \), and taking the cross product, we get:
    • \( \vec{v_D} = L_2sin(\theta)\omega_{BC}~\hat{i}-\omega_AL_1~\hat{j}-L_2cos(\theta)\omega_{BC}~\hat{j} + L_3cos(\phi) \omega_{CD}~\hat{j} + L_3sin(\phi)\omega_{CD}~\hat{i} \)

      • Remember \( \vec{v}_D = 0 \). Separating the vector into \( \hat{i}, \hat{j} \) components we get:

    • \( 0\hat{i} = L_2sin(\theta)\omega_{BC}~\hat{i} + L_3sin(\phi)\omega_{CD}~\hat{i} \)
    • \( 0\hat{j} = -\omega_AL_1~\hat{j}-L_2cos(\theta)\omega_{BC}~\hat{j} + L_3cos(\phi) \omega_{CD}~\hat{j} \)

      • Now we have 2 equations and 2 unknowns. We can solve for \( \omega_{CD} \) now.

      \( \omega_{CD} = \frac{\omega_A L_1}{\frac{L_2cos(\theta)L_3sin(\phi)}{L_2sin(\theta)}+L_3cos(\phi)} \) = 3.6415323071709467 rad/s
