Level 1

    Applying conservation of angular moment:

  • State 1: The moment just before the wheel makes contafct with the bump.
  • State 2: The moment the wheel is rotating over the bump.

    • conservation of angulare Moment: \( H_1 = H_2 \)

  • \( r(mv_1) + I \omega_1 = r' (mv_2) + I \omega_2 \)
  • Here : r' = (r-H). This is becouse when the wheel is roating on the bump to get over it, its rotating about the height of the bum and no long at the base of the wheel.

    • If subsitute \( \omega _2 = \frac{v_2}{r} \) since there ios no slipping we get:

  • \( r(mv_1) + I \omega_1 = r' (mv_2) + I (\frac{v_2}{r}) \)

    • In this equation we have 2 unknows. \( v_1 \) and \( v_2 \), We can use conservation of enerrgy to obtasinanother eqwuation to solve for the unkowns.

    Solving for \( v_2 \):

  • \( v_2 = v_1 (\frac{r m +frac{I}{r} }{(m r' + \frac{I}{r})}) \)

Level 2

    Applying conservation of energy to state 1 and state 3:

  • State 1: we said it was, The moment just before the wheel makes contact with the bump.
  • State 3: This is the moment the wheel just makes it over the entier bump with zero velocity.

    • Conservation of energy says:

  • \( PE_1 + KE_1 + U_{1-3} = PE_3 + KE_3 \)
  • \( 0 + (\frac{1}{2} mv_1^2 + \frac{1}{2} I \omega_1^2 )+ 0 = mgH + 0 \)

    • If subsitute \( \omega_1 = \frac{v_1}{r} \) since there is no slipping we get:

  • \( 0 + (\frac{1}{2} mv_1^2 + \frac{1}{2} I (\frac{v_1}{r})^2 )+ 0 = mgH + 0 \)

    • Solving for \( v_1 \) we get:

  • \( v_1 = \sqrt{\frac{mgH}{\frac{1}{2} (m + \frac{I}{r^2})} } \)
