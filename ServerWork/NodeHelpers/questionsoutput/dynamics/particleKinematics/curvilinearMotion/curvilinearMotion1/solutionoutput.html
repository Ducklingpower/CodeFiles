Level 0

At this instant in time the ball has two acceleration components, A tangential and a normal component. The tangential is given, the normal component is just the centripetal acceleration.

Level 1

Centripetal acceleration is defined by \( A_c = \frac{v^2}{r} \).

we have the velocity which is just \( v_o = 17 \) m/s, all we need is the radius. (r) is the radius of curvature of y(x) at x = 52 meters.

Try to compute the radius to find \( A_c \)

Level 2

    Computing radius at x = 52:

    In order to compute the radius of y(x) at any x we can just use |r| = \( \frac{(1+(\frac{dy}{dx})^2)^{3/2} }{\frac{d^2y}{dx^2} } \)

    Plugging in our equation we get \( |r| = \frac{(1+(\frac{12}{x})^2)^{3/2} }{- \frac{12}{x^2} } \) \( \implies \) \( \frac{(1+(\frac{12} { 52 })^2)^{3/2} }{- \frac{12}{ 52 ^2} } = 243.57089265789614 \) meters

    Now that we have r we can compute the \( A_c \).

Level 3

    Computing net acceleration:

    \( A_c = \frac{v^2}{r} \) \( \implies \) \( \frac{ 17^2 }{ 243.57089265789614 } \)

    A = \( \sqrt{A_t^2+A_c^2} \) \( \implies \) \( \sqrt{ 0.6^2 + (\frac{ 17^2 }{ 243.57089265789614 })^2} = 1.3295912159125054 \)
