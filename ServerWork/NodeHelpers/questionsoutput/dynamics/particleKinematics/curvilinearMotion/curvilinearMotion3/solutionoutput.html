Level 0

try to use \( V = \sqrt{\dot{r}^2+r\dot{\theta}^2} \). Here r in the equation is just the length of the rope, let's call that L.

Level 1

In order to use \( V = \sqrt{\dot{r}^2+r\dot{\theta}^2} \) we need to find every varible besides \( \dot{\theta} \), since that is what we are solving for.

The length of the rope (L) can be found to be \( L = 2rcos(\theta) \) if we take the derivative of L with respect to \( \theta(t) \) we can find the rate at which L changed with the theta.

So, \( \dot{L} = -2rsin(\theta)\dot{\theta} \). Again here our length of the rope represents r in the equation \( V = \sqrt{\dot{r}^2+r\dot{\theta}^2} \) \( \implies \) \( V = \sqrt{\dot{L}^2+L\dot{\theta}^2} \).

Now you can solve for \( \dot{\theta} \).

Level 2

    Solving for \( \dot{\theta} \):

    \( V = \sqrt{\dot{L}^2+L\dot{\theta}^2} \) \( \implies \) \( \sqrt{\frac{v_o^2}{(-2rsin(\theta))^2+(2rcos(\theta))^2} } \)

    \( \dot{\theta} = 1.7307692307692306 \) \( \frac{rad}{s} \).
