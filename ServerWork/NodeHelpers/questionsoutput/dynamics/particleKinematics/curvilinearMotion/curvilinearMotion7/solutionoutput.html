Level 0

This is a curviliner problem, we can take the derivatives of the theta and r equation to obtain the acceloration and veloity in the theta and r direction.

Level 1

Talking derivatives of r:

  • \( r = c_1 t^2 \)
  • \( \dot{r} = 2c_1 t \)
  • \( \dot{\dot{r} } = 2c_1 \)

    • Taking deriavtives of \( \theta \)

  • \( \theta = c_2 t^3 \)
  • \( \dot{\theta} = 3c_2 t^2 \)
  • \( \dot{\dot{\theta} } = 6c_2t \)

    • Now we can solve for velicty and accelortation.

    Level 2

      Solving for Velocity:

    • \( v = \sqrt{ (\dot{r})^2 + (r \dot{\theta})^2 } \)
    • \( v = \sqrt{ (2c_1 t)^2 + ((c_1 t^2) ~ 3c_2 t^2)^2 } \) = 2.8685122509498435 m/s

      • Solving for acceloration:

    • a =\( \sqrt{(\dot{\dot{r} } - r\dot{\theta}^2)^2 + (r \dot{\dot{\theta} } + 2 \dot{r} \dot{\theta})^2} \)
    • a =\( \sqrt{(2c_1 - c_1t^2 (c_2 3 t^2)^2 )^2 + (c_1 t^2 (6c_2 t ) + 2 (2c_1t) c_2 3 t^2)^2} \) = 16.39645748017837 \( m/s^2 \)
