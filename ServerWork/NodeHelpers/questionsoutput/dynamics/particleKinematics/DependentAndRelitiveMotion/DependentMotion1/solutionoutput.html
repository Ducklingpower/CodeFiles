Level 0

The first step is to identify that there are two ropes in this system. We can describe the motion of the two ropes separately.

Level 1

Rope 1:Rope one can be the rope connecting point A and the small pulley.

Equation of motion can be written as, \( L_1 = s_2+s_2 \) \( \implies \) \( 0 = v_2 + v_2 \) \( \implies \) \( v_2 = -v_2 \) \( \implies \) \( v_2 =-v_o \)

Here \( L_1 \) is the length of the rope, and \( s_2 \) is the distance from our datum to the edge of the rope. In this problem, our datum is located in the center of the top pulley.

\( \)

Rope 2: Rope two can connect block B and the ceiling.

Equation of motion for rope 2 can be written as \( L_2 = s_1 + 2(s_1-s_2) \) \( \implies \) \( 0 = v_1+2v_1-2v_2 \) \( \implies \) \( v_1 = \frac{2}{3}v_2 \)

Here \( L_2 \) is the length of rope two, and \( s_1 \) is the distance from the datum to the center of the very bottom pulley.

Level 2

    We can finally solve the velocity of block B by connecting equation 1 with equation 2.

    Connecting the equations we get: \( v_1 =-\frac{2}{3}v_0 \)

    Here \( v_1 \) is the rate at which the length \( s_1 \) changes. And because \( s_1 \) is the length at which the bottom pulley is moving upwards it can resemble the motion of block B.

    Solution: speed of block B is \( v_B =-\frac{2}{3}v_0 \) \( \implies \) \( 5.333333333333333 \) m/s
