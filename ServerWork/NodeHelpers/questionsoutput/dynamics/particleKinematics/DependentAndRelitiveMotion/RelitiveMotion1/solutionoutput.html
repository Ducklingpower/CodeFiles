Level 0

The first step is to break up the velocity of particle A and particle B into x,y components.

Level 1

velocity A: \( V_A = 0\hat{i }-v_a\hat{j} \)

velocity B: \( V_B = (v_Bcos(\theta))\hat{i}+(v_Bsin(\theta))\hat{j} \)

Now it becomes easier to add the components for relative velocity.

Level 2

    To find the direction of relative velocity we can put ourselves in the perspective of particle B. If we were particle B, particle A would appear to be moving to the left and down.

    Relative velocity (\( \hat{i}) \) : \( v_A\hat{i}+v_B\hat{i} \) = \( - (0 + v_Bcos(\theta)) \hat{i} \) = -4.330127018922194 feet/s

    Relative velocity (\( \hat{j}) \) : \( v_A\hat{j}+v_B\hat{j} \) = \( -(v_A+v_Bsin(\theta))\hat{j} \) = -15.5 feet/s
