Level 0

The first step is to break up the system into parts. Break off member CB and AB and draw free body diagrams for each member.

Level 1

Secondly we must solve for the force of the spring. Spring force is equal to \( F_s = k(\Delta{x}) \). In this case \( \Delta{x} \) can by comparing the length of the spring we have now, and the unstretched length of the spring .

Level 2

    Free body diagram for member CB:

  • At pin C we can write Cx and Cy reaction forces as up and left, and at point B we can do the same for Bx and By.
  • We also have to draw the spring force pointing down from point C and we need to draw the weight force point down starting at the middle of the member.

    • Free body diagram for member AB:

  • At pin A we can write the reaction force Ax pointing left. There is no need for Ay since this is a rollar joint.
  • At pin B we have to write the reaction forces opposit to what we did last time so Bx and By are pointing down and right on member AB.
  • We also have to draw the spring force pointing up from pin A and we need to draw the weight force point down starting at the middle of the member.

    • Solving for spring force:

  • Using the equation \( F_s = k(\Delta {x}) \) we can simply just solve for \( \Delta{x} \) since we are given k = 140.25
  • Using trig we can find that the length of the spring is \( 2Lsin(\theta) \) and we are given the unstretched length of the spring so, \( F_s = k(2Lsin(\theta)-0.24) \).
    • Now you can use the free body diagrams to create three equations to solve for your three unknowns. (\( \theta \) , \( B_x \), \( B_y \))

Level 3

    Creating three equations with out free body diagrams:

  • Using member CB we can write a moment equation about point C.
  • \( \Sigma{M}_C = 0 \) \( \implies \) \( -Wcos(\theta)\frac{L}{2}-Lsin(\theta)B_x+Lcos(\theta)B_y=0 \)

  • Using member AB we can write a moment equation about point A.
  • \( \Sigma{M}_A = 0 \) \( \implies \) \( -Wcos(\theta)\frac{L}{2}-Lsin(\theta)B_x-Lcos(\theta)B_y=0 \)

  • Using member AB to write a force equation in the y direction:
  • \( \Sigma{F}_y = 0 \) \( \implies \) \( F_s-W-B_y=0 \)

    • Now you have three equations and three unkowns. You can solve for each varible.
