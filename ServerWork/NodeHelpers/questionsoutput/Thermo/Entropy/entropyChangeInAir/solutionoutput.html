Level 0

The change in entropy of air is calculated by using the following equations and concepts:

  • The air behaves as an ideal gas.
  • The specific volumes at the initial and final states are computed using the ideal gas law.
  • The change in entropy is calculated by applying the entropy change formula for an ideal gas.

Level 1

Step 1: Compute the specific volumes at the initial and final states.

  • Use the ideal gas law: \( V = \frac{mRT}{P} \)

  • For the initial state, \( V_1 = \frac{ 2.2 \cdot 0.287 \cdot 130 }{ 105 }~m^3 \)

  • For the final state, \( V_2 = \frac{ 2.2 \cdot 0.287 \cdot 666 }{ 442 }~m^3 \)

Level 2

Step 2: Use the entropy change formula for an ideal gas:

  • ΔS = \( m \cdot c_p \cdot \ln\left(\frac{T_2}{T_1}\right) + m \cdot R \cdot \ln\left(\frac{V_2}{V_1}\right) \)
  • Where:
    • \( c_p \) = 1.005 kJ/kg·K
    • \( R \) = 0.287 kJ/kg·K
    • \( T_1 \), \( T_2 \) = Initial and final temperatures (K)
    • \( V_1 \), \( V_2 \) = Specific volumes at the initial and final states (m³)

Level 3

Step 3: Combine the equations and solve for entropy change:

  • Plug in \( T_1 = 130~K \), \( T_2 = 666~K \), \( V_1 \) and \( V_2 \) values into the equation.
  • Perform the natural logarithmic operations for \( \ln(T_2 / T_1) \) and \( \ln(V_2 / V_1) \).
  • Calculate ΔS:

    • \( \Delta S = 2.2 \cdot 1.005 \cdot \ln(666/130) + 2.2 \cdot 0.287 \cdot \ln(V2/V1) \)

Level 4

Step 4: Final Result

  • The calculated change in entropy is:
  • ΔS = 3.736 kJ/kg·K
