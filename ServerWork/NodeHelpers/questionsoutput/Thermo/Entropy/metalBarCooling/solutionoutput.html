Level 0

The problem can be analyzed using an energy balance and entropy calculation for the system.

Level 1

  • Assume the system (metal bar and water) is closed and isolated, with no energy transfer to surroundings.
  • Use the energy balance equation to find the final equilibrium temperature \( T_f \):

    • \( \Delta U_{water} + \Delta U_{metal} = 0 \)

  • Expand using specific heat values and masses:

    • \( m_w c_w (T_f - T_{wi}) + m_m c_m (T_f - T_{mi}) = 0 \)

Level 2

    Solving for the final equilibrium temperature \( T_f \):
  • \( T_f = \frac{m_w c_w T_{wi} + m_m c_m T_{mi} }{m_w c_w + m_m c_m} \)

    • Substitute the known values:

  • \( T_f = \frac{(8.4)(4.2)(161) + (0.7)(0.42)(1610)}{(8.4)(4.2) + (0.7)(0.42)} \)

    • Simplify to find:

  • \( T_f = 172.975~K \)

Level 3

    Next, calculate the entropy produced in the process using the entropy balance equation:

    \( \sigma = \Delta S_{water} + \Delta S_{metal} \)

  • For incompressible substances: \( \Delta S = m c \ln\left(\frac{T_f}{T_i}\right) \)
  • Substitute for water and metal:

    • \( \sigma = (8.4)(4.2)\cdot \ln (\frac{ 172.975 }{ 161 } ) + (0.7) (0.42) \cdot \ln (\frac{ 172.975 } { 1610 }) \)

    Insert values and calculate:

  • \( \sigma = 1.875~kJ/K \)

Level 4

    Final Results:
  • Final equilibrium temperature: \( T_f = 172.975~K \)
  • Entropy produced: \( \sigma = 1.875~kJ/K \)
