Level 0

Start by identifying the given data and assumptions for the heat exchanger system.

Level 1

    Given Data:
  • Steam enters the system at \( P_\mathrm{steam} = 300~\mathrm{kPa} \) as a saturated vapor.
  • Water enters at \( T_\mathrm{water} = 80~^\circ \mathrm{C} \) and \( P_\mathrm{out} = 300~\mathrm{kPa} \).
  • Mass flow rate of water: \( \dot{m}_2 = 24.5~\mathrm{kg/s} \).
  • Water leaves as a saturated liquid at \( P_\mathrm{out} = 300~\mathrm{kPa} \).
    Assumptions:
  • Steady-state and adiabatic conditions (\( \dot{Q} = 0 \), \( \dot{W} = 0 \)).
  • Negligible changes in kinetic and potential energy.

Level 2

Determine the enthalpy and entropy values for the given states using thermodynamic tables.

Level 3

    Enthalpy values:
  • \( h_1 = 2725.5~\mathrm{kJ/kg} \) (saturated vapor at \( 300~\mathrm{kPa} \)).
  • \( h_2 = 335.7~\mathrm{kJ/kg} \) (water at \( 80~^\circ \mathrm{C} \)).
  • \( h_3 = 561.2~\mathrm{kJ/kg} \) (saturated liquid at \( 300~\mathrm{kPa} \)).
    Entropy values:
  • \( s_1 = 6.9919~\mathrm{kJ/kg\cdot K} \) (saturated vapor at \( 300~\mathrm{kPa} \)).
  • \( s_2 = 1.0751~\mathrm{kJ/kg\cdot K} \) (water at \( 80~^\circ \mathrm{C} \)).
  • \( s_3 = 1.6698~\mathrm{kJ/kg\cdot K} \) (saturated liquid at \( 300~\mathrm{kPa} \)).

Level 4

Apply the first law of thermodynamics to determine the steam flow rate (\( \dot{m}_1 \)).

Level 5

  • Energy balance: \( \dot{m}_1 h_1 + \dot{m}_2 h_2 = \dot{m}_3 h_3 \).
  • Rearranging: \( \dot{m}_1 = \frac{\dot{m}_3 h_3 - \dot{m}_2 h_2}{h_1} \).
  • Given \( \dot{m}_2 = 24.5~\mathrm{kg/s} \), solve for \( \dot{m}_3 = \dot{m}_1 + \dot{m}_2 \).

Level 6

Calculate the entropy production using the entropy balance equation.

Level 7

    Entropy balance:
  • \( \Delta S_\mathrm{prod} = \dot{m}_3 s_3 - \dot{m}_2 s_2 - \dot{m}_1 s_1 \).

Level 8

Final Answer: The entropy production for the process is \( \Delta S_\mathrm{prod} = 0.985~\mathrm{kW/K} \).
