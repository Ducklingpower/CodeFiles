Level 0

The entropy change during a throttling process for an ideal gas can be expressed as:

\( \dot{m}(s_2 - s_1) = -\dot{m} R \ln\left(\frac{P_2}{P_1}\right) \)

Level 1

Rearrange the equation to solve for the pressure ratio, \( \frac{P_2}{P_1} \):

\( \ln\left(\frac{P_2}{P_1}\right) = -\frac{\Delta S}{\dot{m} R} \)

\( \frac{P_2}{P_1} = e^{-\frac{\Delta S}{\dot{m} R} } \)

Level 2

Substitute the given values:

  • Mass flow rate, \( \dot{m} = 2.2~kg/s \)
  • Entropy change, \( \Delta S = 0.726~kW/K \)
  • Specific gas constant, \( R = 0.287~kJ/(kg \cdot K) \)

\( \ln \frac{P_2}{P_1} = -\frac{ 0.726 }{(2.2 \cdot 0.287)} \)

Level 3

Calculate the pressure ratio:

\( \frac{P_2}{P_1} = e^{-\frac{ 0.726 }{ (2.2 \cdot 0.287)} } \)

Substituting the given values, we find:

\( \frac{P_2}{P_1} = 0.317 \)
