  • The net power output of the cycle is \( W_{net} = 211~\mathrm{MW} \).

Level 1

Use thermodynamic tables to find the enthalpy (\( h \)) and entropy (\( s \)) at key points in the cycle:

  • At state 1: For saturated vapor at \( P_{turbine\_in} = 6~\mathrm{MPa} \), look up the specific entropy \( s_1 = 5.889~\mathrm{kJ/kg\cdot K} \) and specific enthalpy \( h_1 = 2784.3~\mathrm{kJ/kg} \).
  • At state 2: The turbine expansion is isentropic, so \( s_2 = s_1 = 5.889~\mathrm{kJ/kg\cdot K} \). Using the condenser pressure \( P_{condenser\_out} = 0.02~\mathrm{MPa} \):

    Calculate the quality, \( x_2 \):

    \( x_2 = \frac{s_2 - s_f}{s_g - s_f} \)

    Then find the isentropic enthalpy at state 2:

    \( h_{2s} = h_f + x_2(h_g - h_f) = 1936.752~\mathrm{kJ/kg} \)

    Using the isentropic efficiency n = 72 %, \( h_2 \) can be computed

    \( h_2 = h_1 - n(h_1 -h_{2s}) = 2174.066~\mathrm{kJ/kg} \)

  • At state 3: At the condenser outlet, the fluid is saturated liquid. From the thermodynamic tables:

    \( h_3 = h_f = 251.4~\mathrm{kJ/kg} \)

  • At state 4: The pump process is isentropic, so \( s_4 = s_3 = 5.889~\mathrm{kJ/kg\cdot K} \). Using the pump work equation:

    \( h_4 = h_3 + \frac{\dot{W}_p }{\dot{m} } \)

    Calculate \( h_4 = 259.848~\mathrm{kJ/kg} \)

Level 2

Calculate the thermal efficiency (\( \eta \)) of the cycle:

The thermal efficiency is given by:

\( \eta = \frac{W_{net} }{\dot{Q}_{in} } = \frac{(h_1 - h_2) - (h_4 - h_3)}{h_1 - h_4} \)

Substitute the values:

\( \eta = \frac{(2784.3 - 2174.066) - (259.848 - 251.4)}{ 2784.3 - 259.848 } \)

\( \eta = 23.838~\% \)

Level 3

Determine the mass flow rate of the steam:

The mass flow rate is calculated using the net power output:

\( \dot{m} = \frac{\dot{W}_{net} }{(h_1 - h_2) - (h_4 - h_3)} \)

Substitute the values:

\( \dot{m} = \frac{ 211 }{(2784.3 - 2174.066) - (259.848 - 251.4)} \)

Convert to \( \mathrm{kg/h} \):

\( \dot{m} = 1262242.696~\mathrm{kg/h} \)

Calculate the rate of heat transfer into the boiler (\( \dot{Q}_{in} \)):

\( \dot{Q}_{in} = \dot{m}(h_1 - h_4) \)

\( \dot{Q}_{in} = 1262242.696 \cdot (2784.3 - 259.848) \)

\( \dot{Q}_{in} = 885.131~\mathrm{MW} \)

Level 5

Calculate the rate of heat transfer from the condenser (\( \dot{Q}_{out} \)):
