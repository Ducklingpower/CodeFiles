Level 1

Use the isentropic efficiency to determine the enthalpies at key states in the cycle, especially after expansion in turbines and compression in pumps.

Level 2

  • Determining Properties for Each State:
  • State 1: Use steam tables to find enthalpy (\( h_1 \)) and entropy (\( s_1 \)) at the given pressure (\( P_1 \)) and temperature (\( T_1 \)).

    • From steam tables: \( h_1 = 3371.593 \) kJ/kg, \( s_1 = 6.991 \) kJ/kg·K

  • State 2: To determine \( h_2 \), first find the isentropic enthalpy \( h_{2s} \):
    • Use the fact that isentropic expansion means constant entropy: \( s_1 = s_{2s} \).
    • At the lower pressure \( P_2 \), use steam tables to find the corresponding enthalpy \( h_{2s} \) that matches \( s_1 \).
    • If \( s_1 \) falls between saturated liquid and vapor entropy values at \( P_2 \), calculate quality \( x \) using:

      • \( x = \frac{s_1 - s_f}{s_g - s_f} \)

    • Then find \( h_{2s} \) using:

      • \( h_{2s} = h_f + x(h_g - h_f) \)

      With values: \( h_{2s} = 3068.674 \) kJ/kg

    • Finally, apply the isentropic efficiency to find \( h_2 \):

      • \( h_2 = h_1 - \eta_t (h_1 - h_{2s}) \)

      With values: \( h_2 = 3371.593 - \eta_t (3371.593 - 3068.674) = 3068.674 \) kJ/kg

  • State 3: Repeat the process used for State 2 for the second-stage turbine. To determine \( h_{3s} \):
    • Use constant entropy: \( s_2 = s_{3s} \).
    • At pressure \( P_3 \), use steam tables to find the enthalpy \( h_{3s} \) that matches \( s_2 \).
    • Calculate quality \( x \) if \( s_2 \) is between \( s_f \) and \( s_g \):

      • \( x = \frac{s_2 - s_f}{s_g - s_f} \)

    • Find \( h_{3s} \):

      • \( h_{3s} = h_f + x(h_g - h_f) \)

      With values: \( h_{3s} = 2249.76 \) kJ/kg

    • Apply isentropic efficiency to determine \( h_3 \):

      • \( h_3 = h_2 - \eta_t (h_2 - h_{3s}) \)

      With values: \( h_3 = 3068.674 - \eta_t (3068.674 - 2249.76) = 2618.271 \) kJ/kg

  • State 4: As the condenser outputs saturated liquid, use steam tables to find \( h_4 \) and specific volume \( v_4 \) at \( P_3 \).

    • From steam tables: \( h_4 = 121.46 \) kJ/kg, \( v_4 = 0.001004 \) m³/kg

  • State 5: Calculate \( h_5 \) using the pump work equation, assuming isentropic compression:

    • \( h_5 = h_4 + v_4 (P_2 - P_3) \)

    With values: \( h_5 = 121.46 + 0.001004 (P_2 - P_3) = 121.958 \) kJ/kg

  • State 6: Since this is saturated liquid from the feedwater heater, obtain \( h_6 \) and \( v_6 \) directly from steam tables at \( P_2 \).

    • From steam tables: \( h_6 = 640.23 \) kJ/kg, \( v_6 = 0.0010926 \) m³/kg

  • State 7: Use the pump work relation for Pump 2 to find \( h_7 \):

    • \( h_7 = h_6 + v_6 (P_1 - P_2) \)

    With values: \( h_7 = 640.23 + 0.0010926 (P_1 - P_2) = 644.054 \) kJ/kg

Level 3

  • Determine the pump work using isentropic relations:

    • \( h_5 = h_4 + v_4 (P_2 - P_3) \)

    \( h_7 = h_6 + v_6 (P_1 - P_2) \)

  • Calculate the mass extraction fraction (y) at the open feedwater heater:

    • \( y = \frac{h_6 - h_5}{h_2 - h_5} \)

    With values: \( y = \frac{ 640.23 - 121.958 }{ 3068.674 - 121.958 } \)

Level 4

  • Calculate the turbine work per unit mass:

    • \( \frac{\dot{W}_t}{\dot{m}_1} = (h_1 - h_2) + (1 - y)(h_2 - h_3) \)

    With values: \( \frac{\dot{W}_t}{\dot{m}_1} = (3371.593 - 3068.674) + (1 - y)(3068.674 - 2618.271) \)

  • Calculate the pump work per unit mass:

    • \( \frac{\dot{W}_p}{\dot{m}_1} = (h_7 - h_6) + (1 - y)(h_5 - h_4) \)

    With values: \( \frac{\dot{W}_p}{\dot{m}_1} = (644.054 - 640.23) + (1 - y)(121.958 - 121.46) \)

  • Calculate the heat added in the steam generator:

    • \( \frac{\dot{Q}_{in} }{\dot{m}_1} = h_1 - h_7 \)

    With values: \( \frac{\dot{Q}_{in} }{\dot{m}_1} = 3371.593 - 644.054 \)

Level 5

  • Determine the thermal efficiency:

    • \( \eta = \frac{\frac{\dot{W}_t}{\dot{m}_1} - \frac{\dot{W}_p}{\dot{m}_1} }{\frac{\dot{Q}_{in} }{\dot{m}_1} } = 24.56\% \)

  • Calculate the mass flow rate using the net power output:

    • \( \dot{m}_1 = \frac{\dot{W}_{net} \times 3600}{(\frac{\dot{W}_t}{\dot{m}_1} - \frac{\dot{W}_p}{\dot{m}_1})} = 720139.52 \ kg/h \)
