Level 0

Understand the problem and identify known values:

Level 1

We know the following:

  • Heat required to maintain the dwelling temperature: \( Q_H = 896~kJ/day \)
  • Indoor temperature: \( T_{in} = 20~^\circ C \) (or \( T_H = 20 + 273.15~K \))
  • Outdoor temperature: \( T_{out} = 7~^\circ C \) (or \( T_C = 7 + 273.15~K \))

We aim to calculate the minimum theoretical work required by the heat pump.

Level 2

Use the coefficient of performance (\( \gamma_{max} \)) for a reversible heat pump:

\( \gamma_{max} = \frac{T_H} {T_H - T_C} \)

Level 3

Relating the work done to the coefficient of performance:

\( W_{cycle} = \frac{Q_H}{\gamma_{max} } \)

Substitute \( \gamma_{max} \) into the equation:

\( W_{cycle} = Q_H \cdot \left(1 - \frac{T_C} {T_H}\right) \)

Level 4

Perform the calculations:

Substitute the given values:

\( T_H = 20 + 273.15~K \), \( T_C = 7 + 273.15~K \), and \( Q_H = 896~kJ/day \)

\( W_{cycle} = 896 \cdot \left(1 - \frac{ 7 + 273.15}{ 20 + 273.15}\right) \)

Level 5

Final result:

The minimum theoretical work required is: \( W_{cycle} = 39733.92~kJ/day \)
