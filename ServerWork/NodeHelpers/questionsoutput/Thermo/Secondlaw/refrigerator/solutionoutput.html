Level 0

To begin, identify the given parameters and note the temperatures in Kelvin.

Level 1

Convert the temperatures to Kelvin:

  • Freezer compartment temperature: \( T_L = -5 + 273.15 = 268.15~K \)
  • Ambient temperature: \( T_H = 22 + 273.15 = 295.15~K \)

Level 2

Calculate the coefficient of performance (COP) of the refrigerator:

  • Formula: \( \beta = \frac{\dot{Q}_L}{W_{cycle} } \)
  • Substitute the given values: \( \beta = \frac{ 8000 }{ 3200 } = 2.5 \)

Level 3

Calculate the coefficient of performance of the reversible refrigerator:

  • Formula: \( \beta_{\text{max} } = \frac{T_L}{T_H - T_L} \)
  • Substitute the converted values: \( \beta_{\text{max} } = \frac{ -5 + 273.15}{ 22 + 273.15 - (-5 + 273.15)} = 9.931 \)

Level 4

Compare the actual and reversible coefficients of performance:

  • The actual coefficient of performance is \( \beta = 2.5 \)
  • The reversible coefficient of performance is \( \beta_{\text{max} } = 9.931 \)
  • Since \( \beta < \beta_{\text{max} } \), the refrigerator operates at a lower efficiency compared to the ideal reversible refrigerator.
