Level 0

The problem involves finding the minimum theoretical work input required for a heat pump to maintain a dwelling's temperature. Begin by identifying the relevant thermodynamic principles.

Level 1

Using the coefficient of performance (COP) for a reversible heat pump, determine the relationship between the heat supplied to the dwelling and the work input.

Level 2

  • The heat pump maintains the dwelling temperature at \( \mathbf{T_{in} } = 22~^\circ C \).
  • The outside temperature is \( \mathbf{T_{out} } = 10~^\circ C \).
  • The energy required to maintain the dwelling temperature is \( \mathbf{Q_H} = 500000~kJ \).
The relationship for the work input is derived from the COP equation: \[ COP_{max} = \frac{T_H}{T_H - T_C} \] where \( T_H \) and \( T_C \) are the indoor and outdoor temperatures in Kelvin, respectively.

Level 3

  • Rearranging the COP formula, the work input can be expressed as:
    • \[ W_{cycle} = \frac{Q_H}{COP_{max} } \]
  • Substitute \( COP_{max} \) into the equation:
    • \[ W_{cycle} = Q_H \left( 1 - \frac{T_C}{T_H} \right) \]
  • Convert the temperatures to Kelvin:
    • \[ T_H = 22 + 273.15 \quad \text{and} \quad T_C = 10 + 273.15 \]

Level 4

  • Plug in the values for \( Q_H \), \( T_H \), and \( T_C \):
    • \[ W_{cycle} = 500000 \left( 1 - \frac{T_C}{T_H} \right) \]
  • After performing the calculations, the minimum theoretical work input is:
    • \[ W_{cycle} = 20328.646~kJ \]
