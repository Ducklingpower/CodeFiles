Level 0

The problem involves a feedwater heater at steady state with two inlets and one exit. Begin by identifying known values and drawing a schematic.

Level 1

Known:

  • At inlet 1: \( P_1 = 7 \) bar, \( T_1 = 200~ C \), \( \dot{m}_1 = 16~kg/s \)
  • At inlet 2: \( P_2 = 7 \) bar, \( T_2 = 40~ C \), \( A_2 = 22~cm^2 \)
  • At exit: \( P_3 = 7 \) bar, \( \dot{V}_3 = 0.051~m^3/s \)
Find:
  • Mass flow rate at inlet 2, \( \dot{m}_2 \)
  • Mass flow rate at the exit, \( \dot{m}_3 \)
  • Velocity at inlet 2, \( v_2 \)

Level 2

Assumptions:

  • The control volume is at steady state, so \( \frac{dm_{cv} }{dt} = 0 \)
  • One-dimensional flow is assumed at each inlet and the exit

Level 3

Analysis:

  1. Apply the mass rate balance equation: \( \dot{m}_1 + \dot{m}_2 = \dot{m}_3 \)
  2. Find \( \dot{m}_3 \): The volumetric flow rate and specific volume at the exit are given. Use \( \dot{m}_3 = \frac{\dot{V}_3}{v_3} \). With \( v_3 = 1.108 \times 10^{-3}~m^3/kg \) (from Table A-3), \( \dot{m}_3 = \frac{ 0.051 }{1.108 \times 10^{-3} }~kg/s \)
  3. Find \( \dot{m}_2 \): From the mass rate balance, \( \dot{m}_2 = \dot{m}_3 - \dot{m}_1 \)
  4. Find \( \vec{V}_2 \): From \( \vec{V}_2 = \frac{\dot{m}_2 v_2}{A_2} \), convert \( A_2 \) to \( m^2 \) by dividing \( 22 \) cm by \( 10^4 \). \( v_2 = 1.0078\times 10 ^{-3} \) (from Table A-2 at 40 C)

Level 4

Calculations:

  1. Compute \( \dot{m}_3 \): \[ \dot{m}_3 = \frac{ 0.051 }{1.108 \times 10^{-3} } = 46.029~kg/s \]
  2. Compute \( \dot{m}_2 \): \[ \dot{m}_2 = \dot{m}_3 - \dot{m}_1 = 46.029 - 16 = 30.029~kg/s \]
  3. Compute \( v_2 \): \[ v_2 = \frac{\dot{m}_2 \cdot v_2}{A_2} = \frac{ 30.029 \cdot 1.0078 \times 10^{-3} }{\frac{ 22 }{10^4} } = 13.756~m/s \]

Level 5

Final Results:

  • Mass flow rate at inlet 2: \( \dot{m}_2 = 30.029~kg/s \)
  • Mass flow rate at the exit: \( \dot{m}_3 = 46.029~kg/s \)
  • Velocity at inlet 2: \( v_2 = 13.756~m/s \)
