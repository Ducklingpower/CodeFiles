Level 0

Start by applying the energy balance equation for the control volume at steady state.

Level 1

The energy balance equation for steady state is:

\( 0 = \dot{Q} - \dot{W} + \dot{m} (h_1 + \frac{v_1^2}{2} + gz_1 - h_2 - \frac{v_2^2}{2} - gz_2) \)

Assume changes in potential energy (\( gz \)) are negligible.

Level 2

    Steps to calculate:
  • Calculate the mass flow rate (\( \dot{m} \)) using the ideal gas law: \( \dot{m} = \frac{p_1 A_1 v_1}{(R/M) T_1} \).
  • Use specific enthalpy values \( h_1 \) and \( h_2 \) from the given temperatures and ideal gas properties.
  • Substitute known values into the energy balance equation to solve for \( \dot{W} \).

Level 3

    Mass flow rate calculation:
  • \( \dot{m} = \frac{p_1 A_1 v_1}{(R/M) T_1} \)
  • Substitute \( p_1 = 1~bar \), \( A_1 = 0.2~m^2 \), \( v_1 = 6~m/s \), \( T_1 = 290~K \), \( R = 8314~J/(mol\cdot K) \), \( M = 28.97~g/mol \).
  • \( \dot{m} = 1.442~kg/s \)

Level 4

    Substitute into the energy balance equation:
  • Heat transfer rate: \( \dot{Q} = -103~kJ/min = \frac{ -103 }{60}~kW \).
  • Specific enthalpies: \( h_1 = c_p T_1 \), \( h_2 = c_p T_2 \), where \( c_p = 1.005~kJ/(kg\cdot K) \).
  • Kinetic energy terms: \( \frac{v_1^2}{2} - \frac{v_2^2}{2} \)
  • Substitute \( h_1 \), \( h_2 \), and \( \dot{m} \) into \( \dot{W} = \dot{Q} + \dot{m}((h_1 - h_2) + \frac{v_1^2 - v_2^2}{2}) \).

Level 5

Final calculation:

\( \dot{W} = \frac{ -103 }{60} + 1.442 ((291.45 - 452.25) + \frac{(6^2 - 4^2)}{2 \cdot 1000}) \)

\( \dot{W} = -233.553~kW \)
