Level 0

To find the final temperature of the air, we can use the ideal gas law, which states that \( PV = nRT \). Since the number of moles \( n \) and the gas constant \( R \) are constant, we can set up the relationship: \[ \frac{P_1 V_1}{T_1} = \frac{P_2 V_2}{T_2} \]

Level 1

Rearranging the equation to solve for the final temperature \( T_2 \): \[ T_2 = \frac{P_2 V_2 T_1}{P_1 V_1} \]

Level 2

Now substitute the known values: \( T_2 = \frac{ 12 \cdot 22 \cdot 97 }{ 23 \cdot 12 } \)

Level 3

This will give you the final temperature of the air in \( degree F \).
