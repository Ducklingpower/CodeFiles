Level 0

The coefficient of linear expansion ( \( \alpha \)) can be calculated using the formula: \( \alpha = \frac{\Delta L}{L_0 \Delta T} \), where \( \Delta L \) is the change in length, \( L_0 \) is the original length, and \( \Delta T \) is the change in temperature.

Level 1

Here, the change in length is given as \( \Delta L = 9 \) and the original length is \( L_0 = 100 \). The change in temperature is \( \Delta T = 138 - 92 \).

Level 2

Substitute these values into the formula: \( \alpha = \frac{ 9 }{ { 100 \times (138 - 92 )} } \).

Level 3

Therefore, the coefficient of linear expansion is \( \alpha = 0.00019565217391304346 \) in appropriate units.
