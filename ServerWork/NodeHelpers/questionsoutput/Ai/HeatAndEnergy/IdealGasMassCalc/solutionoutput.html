Level 0

To determine the mass of the air, we can use the ideal gas equation: \( PV = nRT \), where \( P \) is the pressure, \( V \) is the volume, \( n \) is the number of moles, \( R \) is the gas constant, and \( T \) is the temperature. Here, we need to express mass (\( m \)) instead of moles (\( n \)).

Level 1

We can relate the number of moles to mass using the equation: \( n = \frac{m}{M} \), where \( M \) is the molar mass of air. Rearranging the ideal gas equation gives us: \( m = \frac{PV}{R \cdot T} \cdot M \)

Given that the characteristic gas constant for air (\( R \)) is provided, we can substitute the known values into the formula, ensuring we have \( P \) in the appropriate units to match \( R \) and \( T \).
