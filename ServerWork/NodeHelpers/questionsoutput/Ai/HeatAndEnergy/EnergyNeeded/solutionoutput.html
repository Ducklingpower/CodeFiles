Level 0

To determine the total heat required \( Q \), we need to consider both the heat required to raise the temperature of the water and the copper container. The heat \( Q \) can be expressed as: \[ Q = Q_{\text{water} } + Q_{\text{copper} } \]

Level 1

The heat required to raise the temperature of the water \( Q_{\text{water} } \) is calculated using the formula: \[ Q_{\text{water} } = m_{\text{water} } \cdot c_{\text{water} } \cdot \Delta T \] where:

  • \( m_{\text{water} } \) is the mass of the water: \( m_{\text{water} } = {\text{density of water} } \times {\text1 } = 8.34 lb \)
  • \( c_{\text{water} } \) is the specific heat capacity of water: 1 BTU/(lb F)
  • \( \Delta T \) is the temperature change \( = { 212 - { 78 } } \)

Level 2

The heat required for the copper container \( Q_{\text{copper} } \) is given by: \[ Q_{\text{copper} } = m_{\text{copper} } \cdot c_{\text{copper} } \cdot \Delta T \] where:

  • \( m_{\text{copper} } \) is the mass of the copper container: 740 lb
  • \( c_{\text{copper} } \) is the specific heat capacity of copper: 0.092 BTU/(lb F)
  • \( \Delta T \) is the same as for water

Level 3

Substitute the values into the equations to find the total heat: \[ Q = (8.34 \times 1 \times (212 - 78)) + (740 \times 0.092 \times (212 - 78)) \]
