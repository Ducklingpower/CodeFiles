Level 0

To find the final pressure of the gas, we can use the ideal gas law for isothermal processes, which states that the product of pressure and volume remains constant: \( P_1 V_1 = P_2 V_2 \).

Level 1

Given:

  • Initial Volume: \( V_1 = 18 ft^3 \)
  • Final Volume: \( V_2 = 14 ft^3 \)
  • Initial Pressure: \( P_1 = 28 psi \)

Level 2

To find the final pressure \( P_2 \), rearrange the equation: \( P_2 = \frac{P_1 V_1}{V_2} \)

Level 3

Substitute in the known values: \( P_2 = \frac{ 28 \cdot 18 }{ 14 } \)

Level 4

Calculate the final pressure to find: \( P_2 = 36 psi \).
