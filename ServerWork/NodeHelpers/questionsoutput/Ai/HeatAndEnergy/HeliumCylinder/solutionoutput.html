Level 0

Start by applying the ideal gas law for this problem, which states that \( PV = nRT \), where:

Level 1

- \( P \) is the pressure of the gas,
- \( V \) is the volume of the gas,
- \( n \) is the number of moles of the gas,
- \( R \) is the ideal gas constant,
- \( T \) is the temperature of the gas in Kelvin.

Level 2

First, we need to calculate the number of moles (\( n \)) of helium using the formula:

  • \( n = \frac{m}{M} \), where \( m \) is the mass of helium and \( M \) is the molar mass of helium (approximately 4 g/mol).

Level 3

Now, substitute \( n \) into the ideal gas law:

  • Rearranging the equation gives us: \( P = \frac{nRT}{V} \).
  • Substitute \( n = \frac{m}{M} \) into the equation:
    \( P = \frac{(\frac{m}{M})RT}{V} \).

Level 4

Finally, we can express the pressure in terms of the known variables:

  • Thus, the pressure of helium in the cylinder can be calculated as:
    \( P = \frac{mRT}{MV} \) = 250.384 psi.
