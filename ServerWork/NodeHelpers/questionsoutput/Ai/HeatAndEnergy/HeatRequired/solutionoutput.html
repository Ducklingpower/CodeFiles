Level 0

The problem requires calculating the energy needed to increase the temperature of a given mass of water from an initial temperature to a final temperature, using its specific heat capacity.

Level 1

We know:

  • Mass of water (\( m \)): 3 kg
  • Initial temperature (\( T_i \)): 17 degree C
  • Final temperature (\( T_f \)): 43 degree C
  • Specific heat capacity (\( C_p \)): 4.18 kJ/kg C

Level 2

The amount of heat required (\( Q \)) can be calculated using the formula: \( Q = m \cdot C_p \cdot (T_f - T_i) \)

Level 3

Substitute the known values into the equation:

  • Calculate the temperature change: \( \Delta T = T_f - T_i = 43 - 17 \)
  • Calculate the heat required: \( Q = 3 \cdot 4.18 \cdot \Delta T \)

Level 4

This calculation assumes no heat loss to the surroundings, and the specific heat capacity (\( C_p \)) remains constant over the temperature range.

Level 5

Therefore, the heat required is calculated as: \( Q = 326.03999999999996 kJ \)
