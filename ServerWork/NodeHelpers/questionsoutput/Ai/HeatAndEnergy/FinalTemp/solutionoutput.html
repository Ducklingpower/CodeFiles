Level 0

The heat energy supplied is given by the formula: \( Q = m \cdot c \cdot \Delta T \), where \( \Delta T = T_f - T_i \) is the change in temperature.

Level 1

Rearranging the formula to find the final temperature, \( T_f \), we have: \( T_f = T_i + \frac{Q}{m \cdot c} \).

Level 2

Substitute the known values to calculate the final temperature:

  • Initial Temperature, \( T_i \): 18 °C
  • Heat Energy supplied, \( Q \): 4355 J
  • Mass, \( m \): 9 kg
  • Specific Heat Capacity of aluminium, \( c \): 900 J/kg°C
Therefore, \( T_f = 18 + \frac{ 4355 } { 9 \cdot 900 } = 18.538 °C \).
