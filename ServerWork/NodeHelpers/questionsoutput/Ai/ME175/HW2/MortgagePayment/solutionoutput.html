Level 0

To calculate the monthly payment and total interest paid on a mortgage, we can use the formula for the monthly payment on an amortizing loan.

Level 1

The formula for the monthly payment (M) is given by: \[ M = P \frac{r(1 + r)^n}{(1 + r)^n - 1} \] where:

  • P = principal amount (initial loan amount)
  • r = monthly interest rate (annual interest rate divided by 12)
  • n = number of payments (loan term in months)

Level 2

In this case, we have:

  • P = 148219
  • r = \( \frac{ 5 }{100 \cdot 12} \)
  • n = \( 15 \cdot 12 \)

Level 3

Substituting these values into the formula gives: \[ M = 148219 \frac{\frac5{100 \cdot 12} (1 + \frac5{100 \cdot 12} )^{(15 \times 12)} }{(1 + \frac5{100 \cdot 12} )^{(15 \times 12)} - 1} \]

Level 4

The total amount paid over the life of the mortgage is given by: \[ \text{Total Payments} = M \times n \] Therefore, the total interest paid can be calculated as: \[ \text{Total Interest} = \text{Total Payments} - P \]

Level 5

Using the values calculated, we can find the monthly payment and total interest paid over the life of the mortgage.
