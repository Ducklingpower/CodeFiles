Level 0

To calculate the monthly payment, we will use the following formula: \[ A = \frac{P \cdot i \cdot (1 + i)^n} {(1 + i)^n - 1} \]

Level 1

Here, the variables are defined as follows:

  • \( A \): Monthly payment
  • \( P \): Loan principal
  • \( i \): Monthly interest rate, calculated as \( i = \frac{r}{12} \)
  • \( n \): Total number of payments, calculated as \( n = \text{loanTerm} \times 12 \)

Level 2

First, calculate the monthly interest rate: \[ i = \frac{\frac{\text{annualInterestRate} }{100} }{12} \]

Level 3

Next, calculate the total number of payment periods: \[ n = \text{loanTerm} \times 12 \]

Level 4

Substitute the values of \( P \), \( i \), and \( n \) into the formula to calculate the monthly payment: \[ A = \frac{P \cdot i \cdot (1 + i)^n}{(1 + i)^n - 1} = \frac{ 300000 \cdot 0.003333 \cdot (1 + 0.003333)^{360} }{(1 + 0.003333)^{360} - 1} = 1432.25\]

Level 5

To calculate the remaining balance after \( k \) payments, we use the following formula: \[ B_k = A \cdot \frac{(1 + i)^{360-t} } {(1 + i)^{360-t} - 1} = 1432.25 \cdot \frac{(1 + 0.003333)^{360-60 } } {(1 + 0.003333)^{360-60 } - 1} = 271342.539 \]

Level 6

Here, the variables are defined as follows:

  • \( B_k \): Remaining balance after \( k \) payments
  • \( t \): Number of payments made

Level 7

To find the total amount paid by the lender, use the formula: \[ \text{Total Paid} = A \cdot t = 1432.25 \cdot 60 = 85935\]

Level 8

To find the principal paid, subtract the remaining balance from the loan amount: \[ \text{Principal Paid} = P - B_k = 300000 - 271342.539 = 28657.46\]

Level 9

Finally, to calculate the interest paid, subtract the principal paid from the total amount paid: \[ \text{Interest Paid} = \text{Total Paid} - \text{Principal Paid} = 85935 - 28657.46 = 57277.29 \]
