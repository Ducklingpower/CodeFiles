To calculate the equivalent present worth (PW) of the cash flows, we can use the formula: \[ PW = \sum_{t=0}^{n} \frac{R_t}{(1 + i)^t} \] where \( R_t \) is the net cash flow in year \( t \), \( i \) is the MARR, and \( n \) is the useful life of the asset.

Level 1

First, we need to determine the net cash flow for each year: \[ R_t = \text{Annual Income} - \text{Annual Expenses} \] Substitute the known variables: \[ R_t = 9752 - 1779 \]

Level 2

Next, we will calculate the present worth for the income generated for each year over the lifespan of the band saw, excluding the salvage value, using the MARR: \[ PW_{income} = \sum_{t=1}^{n} \frac{R_t}{(1 + \frac{ 5 }{100})^t} = (9752 - 1779) \frac{(1 + \frac{ 5 }{100})^n -1}{ \frac{ 5 }{100}(1 + \frac{ 5 }{100})^n } \] and for the salvage value at the end of the lifespan: \[ PW_{salvage} = \frac{ 5000 }{ {(1 + \frac{ 5 }{100})^{n} } } \]
