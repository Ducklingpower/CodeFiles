Level 0

Begin by calculating the kinetic energy of the truck at the start.

Level 1

The kinetic energy (KE) can be calculated using the formula: KE = \( \frac{1}{2}mv^2 \), where \( m \) is the mass in kilograms, and \( v \) is the velocity in meters per second. For this problem, \( m = 5881 \) kg and \( v = 78 \) m/s.

Level 2

  • First, note the weight of the truck is already in kilograms (kg) and the velocity is in meters per second (m/s).
  • Weight in kg: \( m = 5881 \) kg.
  • Velocity in m/s: \( v = 78 \) m/s.
  • Then, calculate KE: KE = \( \frac{1}{2} \times 5881 \times (78)^2 \).
