Level 0

Begin by analyzing the energy conversion during braking.

Level 1

The kinetic energy of the truck is converted into thermal energy in the brakes, which can be expressed as: \( \Delta KE = \Delta Q \).

Level 2

  • The initial kinetic energy can be calculated using: \( \Delta KE = \frac{1}{2} m v^2 \), where \( m \) is the mass in slugs (1 lb = 1/32.2 slugs) and \( v \) is the velocity in ft/s (1 mph = 1.46667 ft/s).
  • The thermal energy absorbed by each brake can be calculated using: \( \Delta Q = m_{brake} c \Delta T \), where \( m_{brake} \) is the mass of each brake, \( c \) is the specific heat, and \( \Delta T \) is the temperature change.

Level 3

  • Convert the truck's speed to ft/s: \( v = 85 /3.6 \) m/s.
  • Calculate the initial kinetic energy: \( \Delta KE = \frac{1}{2} \times m \times v^2 \).
  • Since the kinetic energy is entirely converted to thermal energy in the brakes, \( \Delta KE = \Delta Q_{total} \).
  • The total thermal energy is distributed among all the brakes: \( \Delta Q_{total} = 4 \times \Delta Q_{brake} \).

Level 4

Solve for \( m_{brake} \):

  • \( \Delta Q_{brake} = m_{brake} c \Delta T \)
  • \( m_{brake} = \frac{\Delta KE}{ 4 c \Delta T} \)
  • Plug in the values to find the mass of each brake: \( m_{brake} = \frac{ 5.215 }{ 460 \times 124 } \)
