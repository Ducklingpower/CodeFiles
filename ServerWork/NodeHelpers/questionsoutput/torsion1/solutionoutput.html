Level 0

Compute the net torque that weill be acting on the cross section of AA.

Level 1

The torque acting on the cross section of AA is simply \( \tau_1 \) or \( \tau_2 - \tau_3 \) = \( 24.7 \). Now you can use this value to calcuate the shear stress.

Level 2

    The equation to calculate the shear stress is \( shear stress = \frac{\tau ~ C}{J} \)

    Where J = \( \frac{\pi}{2} ~ R^4 \)

    In solving for the shear stress on the surface of the beam C is equal to the radius of the beam.

    So solution for the first question becomes: \( \frac{\tau_1 ~ 2}{\pi ~ R^3} \)

    Be sure to conver to SI before calcuating.

Level 3

    For the second part, since we are no longer solving for shear stress at the surface fo the beam C does not equal the radius

    C = \( 136 \). So our solution now becomes: \( \frac{\tau_1 ~ 2 ~ C}{\pi R^4} \)
