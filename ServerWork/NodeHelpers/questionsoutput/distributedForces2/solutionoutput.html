Level 0

Draw the freebody diagram. Then write out the equations for \( \Sigma F_x = 0 \), \( \Sigma F_y = 0 \) and \( \Sigma M_A = 0 \). You could also take sum of moments about point A.

Level 1

Replace the distributed force by a single concentrated force acting at the centroid of the distributed force

Level 2

  • By doing \( \Sigma F_x = 0 \implies A_x = 0 \)
  • Replace the distributed force by a single concentrated force.
  • \( \Sigma M_A = 0 \implies B_y (1+ 3) - (12.14)(3)(1+ 3/2) + (6.04)(1.98)(1+ 1.98/2)+ (69)(1+ 3+ 1)= 0 \)
  • By doing \( \Sigma F_y = 0 \implies A_y + B_y + 69 - (12.14)(3) - (6.04)(1.98) = 0 \)

Level 3

  • Solve the moment equation for \( B_y \) to obtain \( B_y = 69.437202 N \)
  • Substitute \( B_y \) and solve for \( A_y \) in the \( \Sigma F_y = 0 \) equation to obtain \( A_y = 48.816402 N \)
