Level 0

First you want to compute the reaction forces at A and B. The first step in doing so is to convert the distributed load into one force concentrated at its centroid.

We can compute the magnitde of the net force of the distributed force to be \( F(L) \). The centroid is simply \( \frac{L}{2} \)

Now that you have the magniude of the force and the location, you can compute the reaction forces.

Level 1

In order to compute the reation forces you have to set up your equilibrium equations.

  • \( \Sigma{F}_y = A_y+B_y-F(L) = 0 \)
  • \( \Sigma{M}_A = B_y (L) - F(L)(\frac{L}{2}) =0 \)

    • Solving for the reaction forces we get:

  • \( B_y = \frac{F(L)}{2} = 15.75 ~kN \)
  • \( A_y = F(L)-B_y = 15.75 ~kN \)

    • With all of the forces acting on the beam you can draw the shear force and moment diagram. You can use the moment diagram to find your max moment.

    Level 2

      The max moment is the area under shear force curve of the positive half.
    • Max moment = \( \frac{A_y(\frac{L}{2})}{2} = 23.625 \) kN*m

      • Moving on to finding the moment of inertia: I = \( \Sigma(\bar{I}+Ad^2) \)

      Where \( \bar{I} = \frac{1}{12}b(h^3) \) and d = distance from sub shape centroid to centrial axis.

    Level 3

      Solving for Inertia:

    • \( \bar{I}+Ad^2 \) for the bottem and top rectangle: \( 2[\frac{1}{12}(L_2)(L_3)^3 + (L_2)(L_3)(\frac{L_1}{2}+\frac{L_3}{2})^2] \)
      • We can just compute \( \bar{I}+Ad^2 \) as if we are only doing it for the top rectangle, then multiply it by 2 since the top and bottem rectangle are geometricly the same.

    • \( \bar{I}+Ad^2 \) for the middle rectangle : \( [\frac{1}{12}(L_3)(L_1)^3] \)
      • We dont need to compute \( Ad^2 \) for the center rectangle since the centreoid of the center rectangle = central axis, so the distance between centreoid of the center rectangle and the central axis will be d = 0

      We can now add up the moment of inertia of the sub shapes to get:

    • I = \( \Sigma(\bar{I}+Ad^2) \) = \( 2[\frac{1}{12}(L_2)(L_3)^3 + (L_2)(L_3)(\frac{L_1}{2}+\frac{L_3}{2})^2] \) + \( [\frac{1}{12}(L_3)(L_1)^3] = 317.035026 ~ \mu m^4 \)

    Level 4

      Lastly you can compute the Max bending stress as :\( \frac{M ~C}{I} \), where C is the distance you want to calculate the bending stress.

      In our case C is the distance form center to the edge, so that will be \( \frac{L_1}{2}+L_3 \)

    • \( \sigma_{max} = \frac{M ~C}{I} = 12.742677208164373 \) Mpa
