Level 0

Draw a free body diagram on member CB and try and solve for the unkowns.

Level 1

When drawing a free body diagram on member CB we can draw the reaction forces on point C as down and left for Cy and Cx. Since member AB is a two force member we can draw the reaction force at point B as a single force point towards B and at an angle that follows a line from point A to B. Lastly you can draw the tension forces causes by the pully and the weight. The tension points left and down coming from the pully.

Level 2

    Solving using member CB:

    Let \( \theta = tan^{-1}(\frac{2_1}{L_2}) \)

  • \( \Sigma{M}_C = 0 \) \( \implies \) \( B_rSin(\theta)L_2+ W(R) - W (L_2+L_3+R) = 0 \)

    • Solving for \( B_R \) (the reaction force on point B) we get:

  • \( B_R = \frac{W(L_2+L_3+R)-W(R)}{Sin(\theta)L_2} \)
    • We can break the reaction force \( B_R \) into \( B_x \) and \( B_y \):
  • \( B_x = cos(\theta)B_R \)
  • \( B_y = sin(\theta)B_R \)
    • Now you can try to solve for \( C_x \) and \( C_y \)

Level 3

    Solving for \( C_x \) and \( C_y \):
  • \( \Sigma{F}_x = 0 \) \( \implies \) \( B_x - C_x -W = 0 \) \( \implies \) \( C_x = W-B_x \)
  • \( \Sigma{F}_y = 0 \) \( \implies \) \( B_y - C_y -W = 0 \) \( \implies \) \( C_y = W-B_y \)
    • Solutions: \( B_x = 729.5555555555557 lb \) \( B_y = 547.1666666666667 lb \) \( C_x = 260.55555555555566 lb \) \( C_y = 78.16666666666674 lb \)
