Level 0

Draw a free-body diagram of the beam after it has made contact with the wall. And compute the normal force at A.

Level 1

Drawing the free body diagram of the bream and doing \( \Sigma{F}_x \), you can compute that the normal force at A is \( N_A = F-N_B \).

Knowing that the max length the beam could extend, create a second equation that models the net change in length.

Level 2

    Equations:
  • \( N_A = F-N_B \)
  • \( \frac{N_A~L_1}{A~E}+\frac{N_B~L_2}{A~E}= L_3 \)
    • With these two equations, we can solve for the normal force at B (\( N_B \)).

Level 3

    Plugging in \( N_A = F-N_B \) into \( \frac{N_A~L_1}{A~E}+\frac{N_B~L_2}{A~E}= L_3 \), you can solve for the \( N_B \)

    Where A = \( \pi(\frac{D}{2})^2 \), and E = \( 200(10)^2 \) Pa

  • When solving it helps to put everything in standard si.

    • \( N_B = \frac{L_3~A~E-L_1~F}{-L_1-L_2} \)
