Level 0

The first step is to compute the normal force on the wall. You can do that by doing \( \Sigma{F}_x = 0 \)

Level 1

By doing \( \Sigma{F}_x = 0 ~\implies F_1 +F_2 -F_3 - N ~\implies N = F_1+F_2-F_3 \)

With all of the forces available you can start breaking the beam into sections:

The first section you should try is the section with length \( L_3 \). Draw a free-body diagram of the cutout section and compute the net force acting on it.

Level 2

    Free body diagram of the cutout section of length \( L_3 \): The forces acting on the beam on the right side is \( F_3 \) pointing left, and the forces acting on the left side are (\( F_1+F_2-N \)) pointing right. The member is in compression.

    Since the member is in compression we know the net change of the segment of length \( L_3 \) is going to be negative.

    Now try and draw the free body diagrams of the segments with lengths \( L_1 \) and \( L_2 \).

Level 3

    After drawing the free body diagrams of all 3 segments you should have come to the conclusion that forces acting on each member is:

  • Section of length \( L_3 \) = \( F_3 \), compression
  • Section of length \( L_2 \) = \( F_2 - F_3 \), compression
  • section of length \( L_1 \) = \( F_1+F_2 - F_3 \), tension

    • Now that we have all of the forces acting on each segment we can sum up how each segment changes in length using this equation. \( \delta = \frac{F~L}{A~E} \)

    Try to calculate all of the lengths and sum them up to get the net change in length.

Level 4

    Calculating the net change in length:

  • \( \delta ~L_3 =\frac{(F_3) ~ L_3}{A ~ 200(10)^9} \)
  • \( \delta ~L_2 =\frac{(F_2-F_3) ~ L_2}{A ~ 200(10)^9} \)
  • \( \delta ~L_1 =\frac{(F_1+F_2-F_3) ~ L_1}{A ~ 200(10)^9} \)

    • Where A = \( \pi (\frac{ 0.045 }{2})^2 \)

    Adding up the change in lengths:

  • \( \delta = -\frac{(F_3) ~ L_3}{A ~ 200(10)^9}-\frac{(F_2-F_3) ~ L_2}{A ~ 200(10)^9}+\frac{(F_1+F_2-F_3) ~ L_1}{A ~ 200(10)^9} \)
  • \( \delta = -217.23667294123402 ~\mu m \)
