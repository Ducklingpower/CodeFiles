Level 0

Try to compute the strain in the z direction.

Level 1

To compute the strain in the z direction you have to first compute the stress.

  • Stress: \( \sigma = \frac{F}{A} \)

  • strain: \( \epsilon _z = \frac{\sigma}{E} \)

    • Now that you have the strain in the longitudinal direction you can use the poisson's ratio to find the strain in the latitudinal direction.

    Level 2

      Using poisson's ratio to find latitudinal strain:

    • \( \epsilon _x = \epsilon _y = \upsilon~\epsilon _z \)

      • Use these strains to compute the change in lengths in all directions.

    Level 3

      Using the equation \( \epsilon = \frac{\delta L}{L_o} \) we can solve for \( \delta L \).

    • \( \delta L_z = \epsilon _z ~ L_1 = 53.40626729037776 ~ \mu m \)
    • \( \delta L_x = \epsilon _x ~ L_2 = -2.019858156028369 ~ \mu m \)
    • \( \delta L_y = \epsilon _y ~ L_3 = -1.0099290780141845 ~ \mu m \)
