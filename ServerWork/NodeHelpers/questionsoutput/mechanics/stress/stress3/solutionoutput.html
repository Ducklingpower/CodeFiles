Level 0

The first step is to draw a free-body diagram of all the forces acting on the weight.

Level 1

There are only three forces, two tension forces are pointing in the direction of \( R_1 \) and \( R_2 \) away from the weight. There is a weight force point straight down. Using the free-body diagram compute the tension forces.

Level 2

    Computing the tension forces:

    Using the FBD and the static equilibrium equation.

  • \( \Sigma{F}_y = 0 ~\implies T_1sin(\theta1) + T_2sin(\theta_2)-W = 0 \)

  • \( \Sigma{F}_x = 0 ~\implies T_2cos(\theta_2) - T_1cos(\theta_1)=0 \)

    • Solving for T_1 and T_2 we get:

  • \( T_2 = \frac{W}{\frac{cos(\theta_2)sin(\theta_1)}{cos(\theta_1)}+sin(\theta_2)} \)
  • \( T_1 = \frac{T_2cos(\theta_2)}{cos(\theta_1)} \)

    • Using the tension forces you can now compute the average normal shear stress.

Level 3

    Computing average normal stress:

    For \( R_1 \):
  • stress =\( \frac{T_1}{A} = 18.535699124427484 \) MPa

    • For \( R_2 \):
  • stress =\( \frac{T_2}{A} = 6.5235115377093305 \) MPa
