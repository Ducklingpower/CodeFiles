Level 0

First break up the component where the shear stress occurs and draw a free-body diagram.

Level 1

For point a-a we can cut off the right blue square and draw a free body diagram on that. There will be a shear stress force acting downwards on the yellow line of a-a. There is also the force \( \frac{F}{2} \) acting upwards on the bottom of the block. Now try and do the same thing but for shear stress b-b.

Level 2

    FBD for shear stress b-b:

    You can cut off the the right side of the purple line creating a grey square. There is a shear force acting on the line b-b pointing upwards. There is also the force \( \frac{F}{2} \) point downwards acting on the top of your block.

    Now that you have your free body diagrams, compute the shear force.

Level 3

    FBD for a-a:
  • \( \Sigma{F}_y = 0 ~ \implies -V_a + \frac{F}{2} =0 \)
  • Va = \( \frac{F}{2} \)

    • FBD for b-b:
  • \( \Sigma{F}_y = 0 ~ \implies V_b - \frac{F}{2} =0 \)
  • Vb = \( \frac{F}{2} \)
    • using the shear force we can compute the shear stress.

Level 4

    Computing shear stress: \( \tau = \frac{V}{A} \)

    Shear stress on line a-a:

  • \( \tau_a = \frac{V_a}{L_1*thickness} = 131.986889892816 \) kPa

    • Shear stress on line b-b:

  • \( \tau_b = \frac{V_b}{L_2*thickness} = 105.58951191425281 \) kPa
