Level 0

The first step is to convert the distributed force into a concentrated force as well as the location of the force.

Level 1

We can convert the distributed force into a concentrated force by computing the area under the curve. So we can compute the area under the curve as F = \( \frac{F_d(L_1)}{2} \). We can also find the location under the curve as xx = \( \frac{L_1}{3} \).

Now draw a free-body diagram of the beam to compute the tension force holding up the beam.

Level 2

    FBD of beam:

    There is an \( A_x \) and \( A_y \) reaction force both pointing in the positive direction at pin A. You can draw the concentrated force (F), xx distance away from point A. Lastly, there is a tension force pointing into the beam at an angle \( \theta \) off the beam on point B.

    Using the free body diagram, try to compute the tension in the rod holding up the beam.

Level 3

    Computing tension:

    Using the free body diagram we can compute the moment about A.

  • \( \Sigma{M}_A = 0 ~\implies F(\frac{L_1}{3})-Tsin(\theta)(L_1+L_2+L_2) = 0 \)
  • Solving for T we get \( T = \frac{F(L_1)}{3sin(\theta)(L_1+L_2+L_2)} \)
    • Now that we have the tension we can draw a free body of the beam from point B to C to solve for the internal forces.

Level 4

    Drawing the free body diagram from point B to C we can draw the normal force pointing right starting from point C. There is a shear force pointing down and a moment at point C. Lastly we can't forget the tension force acting on point B at an angle \( \theta \).

    Using the free body diagram we can compute internal force acting on point C using static equations.

  • \( \Sigma{M}_C = 0 ~\implies M_C -Tsin(\theta)L_2=0 \)
  • \( M_C = Tsin(\theta)L_2 = 95.53591954022987~ Nm \)

  • \( \Sigma{F}_x = 0 ~\implies N - Tcos(\theta)=0 \)
  • \( N = Tcos(\theta) = 71.9448115615176 ~N \)

  • \( \Sigma{F}_y = 0 ~\implies V-Tsin(\theta)=0 \)
  • \( V = Tsin(\theta) = 41.53735632183908~N \)
