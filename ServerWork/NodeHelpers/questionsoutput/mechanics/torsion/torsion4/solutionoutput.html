Level 0

The first step is to compute the net torque acting about the pipe due to the forces.

Level 1

The torque acting on the pipe is \( \tau = F_1(L_1) + F_2 (L_2) \). Now with the torque, you are able to use the shear stress equation.

Level 2

  • Shear stress = \( \frac{\tau ~ C}{J} \)
  • Where J = \( \frac{\pi}{2} (R^4) \). Or for a pipe J = \( \frac{\pi}{2} (R_{out}^4-R_{in}^4) \)

    • Computing shear stress for the outer wall:

  • \( \frac{\tau~R_{out}~2}{\pi(R_{out}^4-R_{in}^4)} =296.99743076190515 \) kPa.
    • Now try and do the same for the inner radius.

Level 3

    Computing shear stress for the inner wall:

  • \( \frac{M~R_{inner}~2}{\pi(R_{out}^4-R_{in}^4)} =212.14102197278942 \) kPa.
