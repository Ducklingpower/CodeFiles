Level 0

In computing the reaction forces at A and B you have to set up your equilibrium equations:

  • \( \Sigma{F}_y = 0 \implies -(F~\frac{L_1}{2})+A_y+B_y =0 \)
  • \( \Sigma{M}_A = 0 \implies -(F~\frac{L_1}{2}) (\frac{3~L_1}{4}) + B_y (L_1)=0 \)
    • Solving the 2 equations we get
  • \( reaction A_y = 13 \) kN
  • \( reaction B_y = 39 \)kN
    • With these forces, you can draw the shear force diagram and conclude that the max shear force is \( B_y \). We can keep that in mind for the final solution.

    Level 1

    You can compute the central axis using the equation \( \bar{y} = \frac{\Sigma y~A}{\Sigma A} \).

    Computing \( \Sigma y~A \) for the top rectangle:

  • \( (L_3+\frac{L_4}{2})(L_2 ~L_4) \)

    • Computing \( Sigma y~A \) for the bottom rectangle:

  • \( \frac{l3}{2} (L_3~ L_4) \)
    • Putting the equation together we get
  • \( \bar{y} = \frac{(L_3+\frac{L_4}{2})(L_2 ~L_4)~+~\frac{l3}{2} (L_3~ L_4)}{(L_2 ~L_4)~+~(L_3~ L_4)} = 85.55555555555557~mm \)
    • Now that we have the location of the central axis with respect to the bottom of the cross-sectional area, we can compute the moment of inertia.

    Level 2

      We can compute the moment of inertia using the equation: I = \( \Sigma(\bar{I}+Ad^2) \). Where \( \bar{I} = \frac{1}{12}b(h^3) \) and d = distance from sub shape centroid to central axis.

      Solving \( \bar{I}+Ad^2 \) for the top rectangle:

    • \( \frac{1}{12}(L_2~L_4^4) + (L_2~L_4)(L_3+\frac{L_4}{2}-\bar{y})^2 \)

      • Solving \( \bar{I}+Ad^2 \) for the bottom rectangle:

    • \( \frac{1}{12}(L_4~L_3^4) + (L_4~L_3)(\bar{y}-\frac{L_3}{2})^2 \)

      • Putting the two together we get:

    • \( I = [\frac{1}{12}(L_2~L_4^4) + (L_2~L_4)(L_3+\frac{L_4}{2}-\bar{y})^2] ~+ ~ [\frac{1}{12}(L_4~L_3^4) + (L_4~L_3)(\bar{y}-\frac{L_3}{2})^2] = 8.933555555555555~\mu m^4 \)

      • Computing Q=\( \bar{y}'~A' \) is similar. In our case, the area we are going to analyze is the top rectangle.

    Level 3

      Computing Q:

    • Q = \( (L_3+\frac{L_4}{2}-\bar{y})(L_4~L_2) = 0.09955555555555555 ~mm^3 \)

      • Notice that Q is the same format as when we computed \( {Ad^2} \) for the top rectangle, just without the square.

      Now we have everything we need to compute the shear max given the fact the welds are \( t = 28 \) m thick.

      The equation for the max shear stress is : \( \tau_{max} = \frac{V~Q}{I~t} \)

      We have all of the variables we need, now we just need to convert everything into standard SI and plugin. (ie, V = max shear force which we calculated to be \( B_y \))

    Level 4

      Computing max shear:

    • \( \tau_{max} = \frac{V~Q}{I~t} = 0.01552200194025024 \)
