The problem involves calculating work done by a tangential force acting on a wheel over several revolutions. Known Variables:
  • The force $\mathbf{F} = {{params.force}}$ N
  • The diameter $\mathbf{D} = {{params.diameter}}$ mm
  • The number of revolutions $\mathbf{x} = {{params.revolutions}}$
 Convert the diameter to radius in meters and find the circumference of the wheel:
  • Radius $r = \frac{\mathbf{D}}{2} = \frac{{{params.diameter}}}{2}$ mm $= \frac{{{params.diameter}}}{2000}$ m
  • Circumference $C = 2 \pi r = \pi \times \frac{{{params.diameter}}}{1000}$ m
 Equation Setup:
  • Total distance traveled by the force (arc length) $ = C \times \mathbf{x} = \pi \times \frac{{{params.diameter}}}{1000} \times {{params.revolutions}}$ m
    Work is calculated as $W = F \times d$, where $d$ is the distance traveled by the force.
  • Therefore, $W = {{params.force}} \times \left( \pi \times \frac{{{params.diameter}}}{1000} \times {{params.revolutions}} \right)$
