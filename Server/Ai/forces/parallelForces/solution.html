Start by understanding the concept of resultant force and how it behaves when forces have the same or opposing directions. In this problem, the line of action for both forces is the same, which simplifies the calculation of their resultant.
    For forces acting in the same direction:
  • The resultant force is simply the sum of the magnitudes of the two forces.
  • Mathematically: $R = F_1 + F_2$, where $R$ is the resultant force.
 When the forces act in the opposite directions:
  • The resultant force is the absolute difference between the two forces.
  • Mathematically: $R = |F_1 - F_2|$, where $R$ is the resultant force.
Note: Absolute value is used to ensure the resultant force has a non-negative magnitude. Ensure to interpret the results correctly:
  • A positive resultant in the same direction indicates that both forces are reinforcing each other.
  • A resultant with zero magnitude, when subtracted, indicates the forces exactly cancel each other out.
