To find the final temperature of the air, we can use the ideal gas law, which states that \( PV = nRT \). Since the number of moles \( n \) and the gas constant \( R \) are constant, we can set up the relationship: \[ \frac{P_1 V_1}{T_1} = \frac{P_2 V_2}{T_2} \] Rearranging the equation to solve for the final temperature \( T_2 \): \[ T_2 = \frac{P_2 V_2 T_1}{P_1 V_1} \] Now substitute the known values: $ T_2 = \frac{ {{params.Pressure2}} \cdot {{params.volumeFinal}} \cdot {{params.T1}} }{ {{params.Pressure1}} \cdot {{params.volumeInitial}} }$ This will give you the final temperature of the air in \( {{params.unitsTemperature}} \).
