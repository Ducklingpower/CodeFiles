First solve for the monthly payment using the equation:

  • A = loan amount $\frac{i(1+i)^n}{(i+1)^n -1 }$

    • Plugging in we can solve the monthly payment to be:

  • A = ${{params.loan}} \frac{ {{params.i}}(1+{{params.i}})^5}{ (1+{{params.i}})^5 -1 } = {{params.A}}$
    •

    BTLCF (Before-Tax-and-Loan Cash Flow):

  • This is the given revenue minus cost, which is constant at {{params.BTLCF}} each year.
    •

    MACRS Deduction:

    Multiply the machine's purchase price {{params.cost}} by the MACRS percentage for the respective year:

  • Year 1: 33.33%
  • Year 2: 44.45%
  • Year 3: 14.81%
  • Year 4: 7.41%
  • Year 5: 0% (fully depreciated).

    • MACRS Deduction = (Machine Cost) $\times$ (MACRS Percentage)

    Book Value:

  • Subtract the cumulative MACRS deductions from the machine's purchase price {{params.cost}}. This tracks the remaining depreciation value of the machine:
  • Book Value at t= (Book value at t-1) - (depreciation at t)
    •

    Loan Interest Payment:

  • Multiply the remaining loan principal at the start of the year by the annual interest rate {{params.rate}}%.

    • Loan Interest Payment = Remaining Principal $\times$ {{params.rate}}%

    Loan Principal Payment:

  • Subtract the loan interest payment from the total fixed annual loan payment {{params.A}}.
  • The principal payment increases each year as the interest decreases.

    • Principal Payment = Total Payment - Loan Interest Payment

    Remaining Principal:

  • Subtract the principal payment from the loan balance at the start of the year. This tracks the outstanding loan balance.

    • Remaining Principal = Previous Principal - Principal Payment

    Taxable Income:

  • Calculate this by subtracting the MACRS deduction and loan interest payment from the BTLCF.

    • Taxable Income = BTLCF - MACRS Deduction - Loan Interest Payment

    Tax:

  • Multiply the taxable income by the tax rate {{params.taxrate}}%.
  • If the taxable income is negative, treat it as a negitive value.

    • Tax = Taxable Income $\times$ {{params.taxrate}}%

    ATCF (After-Tax Cash Flow):

  • Subtract the total loan payment and tax from the BTLCF. This represents the cash available after all expenses and taxes.

    • ATCF = BTLCF - Total Loan Payment - Tax
