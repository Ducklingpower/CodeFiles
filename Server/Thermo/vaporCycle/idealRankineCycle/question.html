Steam is the working fluid in an ideal Rankine cycle. Saturated vapor enters the turbine at a pressure of $\mathbf{P_{turbine\_in} } = {{params.P_turbine_in}}~MPa$ and saturated liquid exits the condenser at a pressure of $\mathbf{P_{condenser\_out} } = {{params.P_condenser_out}}~MPa$. The net power output of the cycle is $\mathbf{W_{net} } = {{params.W_net}}~MW$.

Determine for the cycle:

  • (a) The thermal efficiency, $\eta$.
  • (b) The back work ratio, $\mathbf{BWR}$.
  • (c) The mass flow rate of the steam, $\dot{\mathbf{m} }$ (in $\mathbf{kg/h}$).
  • (d) The rate of heat transfer, $\dot{\mathbf{Q_{in} } }$, into the working fluid as it passes through the boiler (in $\mathbf{MW}$).
  • (e) The rate of heat transfer, $\dot{\mathbf{Q_{out} } }$, from the condensing steam as it passes through the condenser (in $\mathbf{MW}$).
  • (f) The mass flow rate of the condenser cooling water, $\dot{\mathbf{m_{cw} } }$ (in $\mathbf{kg/h}$), if cooling water enters the condenser at $\mathbf{T_{cw,in} } = {{params.T_cw_in}}~^{\circ}C$ and exits at $\mathbf{T_{cw,out} } = {{params.T_cw_out}}~^{\circ}C$.
