First let's solve for acceleration at A.

  • $a_A = a_c + \alpha $ x $ r_{AC} - \omega^2 r_{AC}$ : Where C is the center of the disk
  • Plugging in we get: $a_A = (r \alpha)\hat{i} - \alpha \hat{k} $ x $ [r \hat{j}] - \omega^2 r \hat{j}$
  • Simplifying we get: $a_A = (r \alpha)\hat{i} + \alpha r \hat{i} - \omega^2 r \hat{j}$

    • $~$

  • Magnitude of acceleration at a_A = $\sqrt{(2r\alpha)^2 + (w^2r)^2}$ = {{correct_answers.A}} $m/s^2$

    • Now we can solve for point B and angular acceleration of the bar.

    Solving: $a_B = a_A + \alpha$ x $r_{BA} - \omega_{BA}^2$

  • Here the angular velocity of the bar is zero at this instant.

    • $a_B = a_A + \alpha$ x $r_{BA}$

  • Plugging in and taking the cross product: $a_B = a_A + \alpha_{BA}L_2 \hat{j} + \alpha_{BA} 2r\hat{i}$
  • Plugging in $a_A = (r \alpha)\hat{i} + \alpha r \hat{i} - (\omega^2 r) \hat{j}$ we get: $a_B = (r \alpha)\hat{i} + \alpha r \hat{i} - (\omega^2 r) \hat{j}+ \alpha_{BA}L_2 \hat{j} + (\alpha_{BA} 2r)\hat{i}$

    • Breaking into i and j components we get:

  • $a_B \hat{i} = 2r\alpha_{BA} + 2r\alpha$
  • $0 \hat{j}=-\omega^2 r + \alpha_{BA} L_1$

    • Here we have 2 equations and 2 unknowns.
