The first step in solving these types of problems is to write out the equation of velocity and Identify what you have and don't have. Equation of velocity at point C:
  • $\vec{v_c} = \vec{v_B} + \omega_{CB} $ x $ \vec{L_2} $

    • Here he can see that we don't know what the velocity of $\vec{v_B}$ is, but we can easily find it.
      Solving for $\vec{v_B}$:
    • $\vec{v_B} = \omega_{A} $ x $ \vec{L_1} $

      • Now we can plug in $\vec{v_B}$ into $\vec{v_c} = \vec{v_B} + \omega_{CB} $ x $ \vec{L_2} $. Plugging in we get and taking the cross product we get:

    • $\vec{v_c} = -L_1sin(\theta)\omega_{A}~\hat{i}+L_1cos(\theta)\omega_A ~\hat{j} - L_2cos(\phi)\omega_{CB} ~\hat{j}- L_2sin(\phi)\omega_{CB}~\hat{i} $

      • Now we can solve for the velocity by breaking up $\vec{v_c}$ into its $\hat{i},\hat{j}$ components

      Breaking up the $\vec{v_c}$ into its components:

    • $v_c\hat{i} = -L_1sin(\theta)\omega_{A}~\hat{i}- L_2sin(\phi)\omega_{CB}~\hat{i}$
    • $0{j} = L_1cos(\theta)\omega_A ~\hat{j} - L_2cos(\phi)\omega_{CB} ~\hat{j}$
      • Now we have 2 equations and 2 unknowns.
