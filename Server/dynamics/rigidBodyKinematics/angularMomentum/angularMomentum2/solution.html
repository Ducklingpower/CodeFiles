Here we can use conservation of angular momentum. Where state 1 is the moment the bullet impacts the slender rod, and state 2 is the moment just after the rod starts moving as a result of the impact.

    Applying angular momentum conservation we get: ($H_1 = H_2$)

  • $(m_b v_{b1})cos(\theta) L_1 = (m_b v_{b2})L_1 + (m_g v_{g2})\frac{L_1 + L_2}{2} + I \omega_2$

    • Here I = $\frac{1}{12} m L^2 ~\implies ~ \frac{1}{12} m (L_1 + L_2)^2 $. Also, $m_g$ is the center of mass of the slender rod.

    We can substitute $v_{b2}$ with $\omega_2 L_1$. Doing that we get:

  • $(m_b v_{b1})cos(\theta) L_1 = (m_b \omega_2 L_1 )L_1 + (m_g v{g2})\frac{L_1 + L_2}{2} + I \omega_2$

    • Now we can solve for $\omega_2$

    Solving for $\omega_2$ we get:

  • $\omega_2 = \frac{(m_b v_{b1}) cos(\theta) L_1 }{m_b L_1^2 ~+ ~m_g (\frac{L_1 + L_2 }{2})^2 ~+~ \frac{1}{12} m_g (L_1 + L_2 )^2}$ = {{correct_answers.w}} $rad/s$
