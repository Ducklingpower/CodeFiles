The first step is to draw the free-body diagram for the wheel. There should be 5 forces acting on the wheel.

Free body diagram of the wheel:

  • Weight force pulling down from the center
  • Tension force pulling up along the rod
  • Normal force pushing right at the contact area of the wall and the wheel.
  • friction force pulling down at the contact area of the wall and the wheel.
  • force pulling down

    • Now we can write out equations of motion to solve for the angular acceleration.

      $\Sigma{F_x} = 0 ~\implies~ N-Tcos(\theta) = 0 ~\implies~ N = Tcos(\theta)$

      $\Sigma{F_y} = 0 ~\implies~ Tsin(\theta) - w - \mu_k N - F = 0$

      $\Sigma{M_G} = I\alpha ~\implies ~ I \alpha = r \mu N - F r$

      Now we have 3 equations and 3 unknowns. ($\alpha T N$)

      Note: $I = k^2 m $ and $\theta = tan^{-1}(\frac{L}{r})$>

      Solving for alpha:

      Plug in, $N = Tcos(\theta)$ , into , $Tsin(\theta) - w - \mu_k N - F = 0$ , and solve for T

    • $T = \frac{F + w }{sin(\theta) - \mu_kcos(\theta)}$

      • $~$

      Now we can plug in T into $N = Tcos(\theta)$

    • $N = \frac{Fcos(\theta) + wcos(\theta) }{sin(\theta) - \mu_kcos(\theta)}$

      • $~$

      Lastly we can plug in N and $I = K^2m$ into $I \alpha = r \mu N - F r$ and solve for $\alpha$

    • $\alpha = \frac{r \mu_k Fcos(\theta) + r \mu_k m g cos(\theta)}{k^2 m sin(\theta) - k^2 m \mu_k cos(\theta)} - \frac{Fr}{k^2 m} = {{correct_answers.a}} \frac{rad}{s^2}$
