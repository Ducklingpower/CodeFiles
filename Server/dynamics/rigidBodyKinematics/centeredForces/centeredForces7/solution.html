The first step is to draw a free-body diagram of the system so that we are able to sum the forces.

Summing forces:

  • $\Sigma{F}_n ~\implies ~\omega^2 L m = N_x$. At the instant, the flywheel is released angular velocity is zero so, $N_x = 0$
  • $\Sigma{F}_t ~\implies ~(\alpha L) m = mg - N_y$
  • $\Sigma{M}_G ~\implies ~ I_G \alpha = N_y L$
    • Here we have 2 equations and two unknowns: $N_y$ and $\alpha$

      Solving we get:

    • $N_y = \frac{mg} {\frac{L^2}{k^2} +1 } = {{correct_answers.ry}}~N$
    • $\alpha = \frac{gL}{L^2 + k^2} = {{correct_answers.alpha}}~rad/s^2$
