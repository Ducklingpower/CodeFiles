The first step is to draw a free-body diagram of the horizontal beam. Take note that there is both tangential acceleration and normal acceleration.

Summing forces in normal and tangential directions:

  • $\Sigma{F}_n ~\implies ~\omega^2L_1 m = N_1 + N_2 - m g cos(\theta)$

    • Here $N_1$ and $N_2$ are the normal reaction forces of the two support beams.

  • $\Sigma{F}_t ~\implies ~a_t m = mgsin(\theta)$
  • $\Sigma{M}_G ~\implies ~ 0 = N_2 cos(\theta) \frac{L_2}{2} - N_1 cos(\theta) \frac{L_2}{2}$
    • Here we have 3 equations and three unknowns: $N_1,~N_2,$ and $a_t$

      Solving:

    • From moment equation: $N_1 = N_2$
    • From $\Sigma{F}_n$ equation: $N_1 = \frac{\omega^2 L_1 m +mg cos(\theta)}{2} = {{correct_answers.N}}~ kN$
    • From $\Sigma{F}_t$ equation: $a_t = sin(\theta) g = {{correct_answers.a}} ~ m/s^2$
