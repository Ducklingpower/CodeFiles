The first step is to draw a free-body diagram and identify the motion of the center of mass.

Identifying the motion of the center of mass:

  • Since the plate is fixed to the pin at A, the place is only allowed to rotate in a circular motion about pin A.
  • We can say that there will be acceleration in both the x and y directions in order to meet the circular motion requirement.
  • So there will be a force in both the x and y direction since the plate has a mass.

    • free body diagram:

  • $\Sigma{F_x} = ma ~\implies~ A_x = m a_x$
  • $\Sigma{F_y} = ma ~\implies~ A_y - w = m a_y$
  • $\Sigma{M_a} = I \alpha ~\implies~ w(r~cos(45)) = I \alpha$

    • r = $\sqrt{(\frac{L}{2})^2 + (\frac{L}{2})^2}$ this is just the vector from point A to the center of mass (center of the plate).

    I = $\frac{m}{12}(L^2+L^2) + mr^2$ . We have to add the ($mr^2$) to the moment of inertia of a square plate since we are taking a moment about point A and not the center of mass.

    Now you can solve.

      Solving for $\alpha $ we get:

    • $\alpha = \frac{w~rcos(45)}{I} = {{params.a}} rad/s^2$

      • Now that we have $\alpha$ we can find the acceleration in both the x and y directions.

    • $a_x = \alpha r*cos(45)$
    • $a_y = \alpha r*sin(45)$

      • we can substitute $a_x$ and $a_y$ in our force equartions:

    • $A_x = m a_x ~ \implies~ A_x = m \alpha r*cos(45)$ = {{correct_answers.Ax}} N
    • $A_y = w - m a_y ~ \implies~ A_y =w - m \alpha r*sin(45)$ = {{correct_answers.Ay}} N
