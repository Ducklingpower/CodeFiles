The first step is to write out the acceleration of A

Writing out the acceleration of A:

  • $\vec{a_A} = \vec{a_G} + \alpha~\times~ [0\hat{i} + (\frac{L}{2})\hat{j}]$, Computing the cross product we get:
  • $\vec{a_A} = \vec{a_G} - \alpha(\frac{L}{2})~\hat{i}$

    • Breaking up the equation into the i and j components:

  • $a_A ~\hat{i} = a_G ~\hat{i} - (\frac{L}{2}) \alpha~ \hat{i}$
  • $a_A ~\hat{j} = 0$, The system can't move up or down with the given forces.

    • Looking at the $\hat{i}$ equations we need to find the acceleration of $a_G$ and angular acceleration $\alpha$.

      Solving for $a_G$ and $\alpha$.

    • $\Sigma{F_x} = m~a_G ~\implies~ F-(\mu w) = m~a_G $
    • $a_G = \frac{F-\mu w}{m}$

      • $~$

    • $\Sigma{M_G} = I_G \alpha ~\implies~ (\frac{L}{2})(\mu w - F) = I_G \alpha$
    • $\alpha = \frac{(\frac{L}{2})(\mu w - F)}{I}$. Here for this rod $I = \frac{m}{12}L^2$. Plugging in we get:
    • $\alpha = \frac{6(\mu w -F)}{mL}$

      • Now that we have $a_G$ and $\alpha$, we can plut them into our $a_A \hat{i}$ equation .

      Solving for $a_A$

    • We have, $a_A \hat{i} = a_G \hat{i} - (\frac{L}{2}) \alpha \hat{i}$. Plugging in $a_G$ and $\alpha$ we get:

      • $~$

    • $a_A ~\hat{i} = (\frac{F-\mu w}{m})~ \hat{i} - (\frac{L}{2})~ \frac{6(\mu w - F)}{mL}~ \hat{i}$ = {{correct_answers.a}} $m/s^2$
