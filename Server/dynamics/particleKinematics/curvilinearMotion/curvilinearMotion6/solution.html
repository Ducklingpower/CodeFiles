Let starting acceleration be $a_i$ and let the final acceleration be $a_f$. The goal is to compute the time needed to obtain $a_f$

Let final acceleration be $a_f = \sqrt{(a_n)^2 + (a_i)^2} $

Where $a_n$ = $\frac{a_i~t}{r}$ ,plugging in we get:

  • $a_f = \sqrt{(\frac{a_i~t}{r})^2 + (a_i)^2} $

    • Now allow have to do is solve for $t$

      Solving for $a_f$:

    • $t = \sqrt{\sqrt{a_f^2 - a_i^2} \frac{r} {a_i^2} } = {{correct_answers.t}}~s$
