First separate the problem into two components. Up and down and left and right. In the Horizontal direction, the velocity will be constant, and in the vertical direction, the acceleration will be constant pointing downwards.
    Writing equations of motion for the horizontal and verticle components.

    Horizontal: $ v = \frac{D}{t} $ $\implies$ $ vcos(\theta + \phi) = \frac{Dcos(\theta)}{t} $ $\implies$ $D = \frac{vcos(\theta+\phi)t}{cos(\theta)}$

    Vertical: $D = vt+\frac{1}{2}at^2$ $\implies$ $Dsin(\theta) = vsin(\theta+\phi)t-\frac{1}{2}gt^2$
    You now have two equations and two unknowns. You can plug in $D = \frac{vcos(\theta+\phi)t}{cos(\theta)}$ into $Dsin(\theta) = vsin(\theta+\phi)t-\frac{1}{2}gt^2$

    After plugging in D you should get $0 = [-\frac{vcos(\theta+\phi)}{cos(\theta)}sin(\theta) +vsin(\theta+\phi)]t-\frac{1}{2}gt^2$
